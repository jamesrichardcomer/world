
1. **Abraham (c. 2000 BC)**
    - Patriarch whose covenant with God founded the monotheistic traditions of Judaism, Christianity, and Islam.
2. **Moses (c. 1350 BC)**
    - Prophet who led the Israelites out of Egyptian bondage and received the Ten Commandments on Mount Sinai.
3. **Laozi (c. 600 BC)**
    - Legendary Chinese sage whose Tao Te Ching founded Taoism, teaching harmony with nature's effortless way.
4. **Siddhartha Gautama (c. 563–483 BC)**
    - Indian prince who became the Buddha, teaching the path to enlightenment and freedom from suffering.
5. **Jesus Christ (c. 4 BC–c. 30 AD)**
    - Jewish teacher whose life, death, and resurrection founded Christianity, now the world's largest religion.
6. **Saint Paul the Apostle (c. 5–c. 64 AD)**
    - Former persecutor turned apostle who spread Christianity throughout the Roman Empire through tireless missionary journeys.
7. **Muhammad (c. 570–632)**
    - Prophet of Islam who received the Quran and united Arabia, launching a faith that now spans the globe.
8. **Martin Luther (1483–1546)**
    - German monk whose Ninety-Five Theses sparked the Protestant Reformation, splitting Western Christianity forever.
9. **John Calvin (1509–1564)**
    - French reformer whose theology of predestination and church discipline shaped Protestant traditions from Geneva to America.
10. **Joseph Smith (1805–1844)**
    - American prophet who founded the Latter-day Saint movement after reporting visions and translating the Book of Mormon.
