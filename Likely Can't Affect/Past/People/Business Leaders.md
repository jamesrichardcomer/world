
1. **Andrew Carnegie (1835–1919)**
    - Scottish-American steel magnate who built an industrial empire, then gave away his fortune to fund libraries and education.
2. **John D. Rockefeller (1839–1937)**
    - Founder of Standard Oil who became America's first billionaire and pioneered modern philanthropy.
3. **Henry Ford (1863–1947)**
    - Automaker who revolutionized manufacturing with the assembly line, making cars affordable for ordinary Americans.
4. **Walt Disney (1901–1966)**
    - Animation pioneer who created Mickey Mouse and built an entertainment empire from cartoons to theme parks.
5. **Warren Buffett (1930–)**
    - The "Oracle of Omaha" whose value investing strategy made Berkshire Hathaway one of the world's most valuable companies.
6. **Bill Gates (1955–)**
    - Microsoft co-founder who put a PC on every desk, then turned to philanthropy to tackle global health and poverty.
7. **Steve Jobs (1955–2011)**
    - Apple visionary who transformed personal computing, animated films, music, and mobile phones with relentless design focus.
8. **Jeff Bezos (1964–)**
    - Amazon founder who grew an online bookstore into the world's largest retailer and cloud computing giant.
9. **Elon Musk (1971–)**
    - Serial entrepreneur pushing boundaries in electric vehicles, space exploration, and neural interfaces.
