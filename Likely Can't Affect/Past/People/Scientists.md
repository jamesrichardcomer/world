
1. **Nicolaus Copernicus (1473–1543)**
    - Polish astronomer who placed the sun at the center of the cosmos, launching the Scientific Revolution.
2. **Galileo Galilei (1564–1642)**
    - Italian astronomer who turned his telescope skyward, championed heliocentrism, and clashed with the Church.
3. **Johannes Kepler (1571–1630)**
    - German mathematician who discovered the laws of planetary motion, proving orbits are ellipses, not circles.
4. **Isaac Newton (1643–1727)**
    - English genius who unified physics with laws of motion and gravity, invented calculus, and revolutionized optics.
5. **David Hume (1711–1776)**
    - Scottish empiricist who challenged causation, miracles, and religious belief with rigorous skepticism.
6. **Michael Faraday (1791–1867)**
    - Self-taught English scientist who discovered electromagnetic induction, making electric motors and generators possible.
7. **Charles Darwin (1809–1882)**
    - English naturalist whose theory of evolution by natural selection transformed our understanding of life on Earth.
8. **Louis Pasteur (1822–1895)**
    - French chemist who proved germ theory, invented pasteurization, and developed vaccines that saved countless lives.
9. **Nikola Tesla (1856–1943)**
    - Serbian-American inventor who pioneered alternating current, wireless transmission, and countless electrical innovations.
10. **Albert Einstein (1879–1955)**
    - German physicist who reimagined space, time, and energy with relativity, becoming the icon of modern genius.
11. **Alan Turing (1912–1954)**
    - British mathematician who cracked Nazi codes and laid the foundations for computer science and artificial intelligence.
