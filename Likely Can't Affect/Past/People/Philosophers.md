
1. **Confucius (551–479 BC)**
    - Chinese sage whose teachings on ethics, family, and governance shaped East Asian civilization for millennia.
2. **Socrates (c. 470–399 BC)**
    - Athenian gadfly who questioned everything and founded Western philosophy through relentless dialogue.
3. **Plato (c. 428–348 BC)**
    - Student of Socrates who founded the Academy and explored reality, justice, and the ideal state in timeless dialogues.
4. **Aristotle (384–322 BC)**
    - Plato's student who systematized logic, ethics, politics, and natural science, tutoring Alexander the Great along the way.
5. **Thomas Aquinas (1225–1274)**
    - Dominican friar who harmonized Aristotelian philosophy with Christian theology, shaping Catholic thought for centuries.
6. **René Descartes (1596–1650)**
    - French mathematician who doubted everything except his own thinking, launching modern philosophy with "I think, therefore I am."
7. **John Locke (1632–1704)**
    - English philosopher whose ideas on natural rights and government by consent inspired democratic revolutions worldwide.
8. **Jean-Jacques Rousseau (1712–1778)**
    - Genevan thinker who championed the social contract and inspired both the French Revolution and Romantic movement.
9. **Immanuel Kant (1724–1804)**
    - German philosopher who revolutionized epistemology and ethics, arguing that morality flows from reason alone.
10. **Karl Marx (1818–1883)**
    - German economist whose critique of capitalism and vision of communist revolution transformed global politics.
11. **Friedrich Nietzsche (1844–1900)**
    - German philosopher who declared "God is dead" and challenged Western morality with radical ideas about power and meaning.
