
1. **Early Homo Sapiens (300,000–100,000 years ago)**
   - Small hunter-gatherer bands totaling perhaps 10,000 individuals scattered across Africa.

2. **Out of Africa Migration (70,000–60,000 years ago)**
   - Human population bottleneck reduces to as few as 3,000–10,000 individuals before spreading globally.

3. **Agricultural Revolution (10,000 BCE)**
   - Farming enables population growth from roughly 5 million to 50 million as humans settle in permanent communities.

4. **Bronze Age Civilizations (3000–1000 BCE)**
   - Urban centers in Mesopotamia, Egypt, and Indus Valley support growing populations reaching 100 million globally.

5. **Classical Antiquity Peak (1 CE)**
   - World population reaches approximately 300 million with major concentrations in Roman and Han empires.

6. **Medieval Population Recovery (1000–1300 CE)**
   - European and Asian populations grow during agricultural expansion and favorable climate conditions.

7. **Black Death Pandemic (1347–1353)**
   - Bubonic plague kills 75–200 million people, reducing global population by up to one-third.

8. **Columbian Exchange (1492 onwards)**
   - European diseases devastate indigenous American populations, killing an estimated 90% within a century.

9. **Global Population Reaches 1 Billion (1804)**
   - First time human population hits this milestone after thousands of years of gradual growth.

10. **Industrial Revolution Demographic Transition (1800s–1900s)**
    - Improved sanitation, medicine, and food production trigger rapid population growth in industrializing nations.

11. **World Population Reaches 2 Billion (1927)**
    - Doubling time accelerates to just 123 years as public health advances reduce mortality.

12. **Post-WWII Baby Boom (1946–1964)**
    - Unprecedented birth rates in Western nations following war's end, reshaping generational demographics.

13. **Green Revolution (1960s–1970s)**
    - Agricultural innovations prevent predicted famines and enable population growth in developing nations.

14. **China's One-Child Policy (1980–2015)**
    - Government mandate limits family size, preventing an estimated 400 million births over 35 years.

15. **World Population Reaches 7 Billion (2011)**
    - Milestone achieved as Asia and Africa drive continued growth despite declining fertility in developed nations.

16. **Demographic Transition Completion (2000s–Present)**
    - Fertility rates decline below replacement level in most developed countries, aging populations rapidly.

17. **Urbanization Milestone (2007)**
    - For the first time in history, more than half of humanity lives in cities rather than rural areas.

18. **World Population Reaches 8 Billion (2022)**
    - Population growth slowing as global fertility rates decline, with peak population projected mid-century.

19. **Aging Population Crisis (2020s–Present)**
    - Developed nations face challenges of shrinking working-age populations supporting growing elderly demographics.

20. **Projected Population Peak (2080s)**
    - UN projections suggest global population will peak around 10–11 billion before potentially declining.
