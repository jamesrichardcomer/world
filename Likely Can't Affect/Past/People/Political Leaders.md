
1. **Alexander the Great (356–323 BC)**
    - Macedonian conqueror who built an empire from Greece to India by age thirty, spreading Hellenistic culture across three continents.
2. **Julius Caesar (100–44 BC)**
    - Roman general who conquered Gaul, crossed the Rubicon, and remade the Republic before his assassination sparked civil war.
3. **Charlemagne (742–814)**
    - Frankish king crowned Holy Roman Emperor who united much of Western Europe and sparked a cultural renaissance.
4. **Genghis Khan (c. 1162–1227)**
    - Mongol founder who conquered the largest contiguous land empire in history, connecting East and West through conquest and trade.
5. **George Washington (1732–1799)**
    - Revolutionary commander who led America to independence, then set democratic precedents as the nation's first president.
6. **Napoleon Bonaparte (1769–1821)**
    - French military genius who crowned himself emperor, conquered Europe, and spread revolutionary ideals before final defeat at Waterloo.
7. **Abraham Lincoln (1809–1865)**
    - Self-taught lawyer who preserved the Union, ended slavery, and became America's most revered president before his assassination.
8. **Winston Churchill (1874–1965)**
    - British bulldog who rallied his nation against Nazi Germany with stirring oratory and stubborn resolve.
9. **Franklin D. Roosevelt (1882–1945)**
    - Four-term president who led America through the Depression and World War II, transforming the role of government.
