
1. **Alexander's Eastern Campaigns (334–323 BCE)**
   - Macedonian conquest reaches India, opening routes between Mediterranean and Asia.

2. **Viking Discovery of North America (c. 1000 CE)**
   - Leif Erikson establishes settlement in Newfoundland, five centuries before Columbus.

3. **Marco Polo's Journey to China (1271–1295)**
   - Venetian merchant travels Silk Road documenting Asian wonders for Europe.

4. **Portuguese Exploration of Africa (1420s–1480s)**
   - Henry the Navigator sponsors expeditions mapping African coast, establishing trade routes.

5. **Columbus Reaches the Americas (1492)**
   - Genoese navigator's Caribbean landfall begins European colonization of New World.

6. **Vasco da Gama Reaches India (1498)**
   - Portuguese explorer completes sea route from Europe to India, revolutionizing spice trade.

7. **Balboa Crosses Panama (1513)**
   - Spanish conquistador becomes first European to see Pacific Ocean from the Americas.

8. **Magellan's Circumnavigation (1519–1522)**
   - First voyage around the world proves Earth's spherical nature and Pacific's vast size.

9. **Cortés Conquers Aztec Empire (1519–1521)**
   - Spanish conquistador topples Mexica civilization, opening Mexico to European colonization.

10. **Jacques Cartier Explores Canada (1534–1542)**
    - French navigator claims St. Lawrence region, establishing France's North American presence.

11. **Francis Drake Circumnavigates Globe (1577–1580)**
    - English privateer completes second circumnavigation, raiding Spanish colonies along the way.

12. **Cook Maps the Pacific (1768–1779)**
    - British captain's three voyages chart Pacific, Australia, and New Zealand with scientific precision.

13. **Lewis and Clark Expedition (1804–1806)**
    - American explorers traverse continent to Pacific, mapping Louisiana Purchase territory.

14. **Humboldt's South American Expedition (1799–1804)**
    - Prussian naturalist's scientific exploration lays foundations for biogeography and ecology.

15. **Livingstone Explores Africa (1841–1873)**
    - Scottish missionary maps vast regions of African interior, seeking sources of the Nile.

16. **Stanley Finds Livingstone (1871)**
    - Welsh journalist's expedition encounters lost explorer, opening Congo region to European exploitation.

17. **Peary Reaches North Pole (1909)**
    - American explorer claims first arrival at North Pole, though claim remains disputed.

18. **Amundsen Reaches South Pole (1911)**
    - Norwegian expedition wins race to South Pole, beating Scott's tragic British team.

19. **First Moon Landing (1969)**
    - Apollo 11 astronauts become first humans to walk on another celestial body.

20. **Mars Rover Exploration (1997–present)**
    - Robotic rovers explore Martian surface, searching for signs of past life and water.
