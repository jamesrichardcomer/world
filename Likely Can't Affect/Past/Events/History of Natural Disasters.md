
1. **Thera Eruption (c. 1600 BCE)**
   - Massive volcanic explosion devastates Minoan civilization on Santorini, possibly inspiring Atlantis legend.

2. **Black Death Pandemic (1347–1353)**
   - Bubonic plague spreads across Eurasia, killing 75–200 million people and reshaping medieval society.

3. **Great Fire of London (1666)**
   - Five-day inferno destroys 13,000 houses and 87 churches, transforming city planning and building codes.

4. **Great Lisbon Earthquake (1755)**
   - Earthquake, tsunami, and fires destroy Portuguese capital, killing up to 100,000 and shaking Enlightenment optimism.

5. **Tambora Eruption (1815)**
   - Indonesian volcano's massive explosion causes "Year Without a Summer," triggering global crop failures and famine.

6. **Irish Potato Famine (1845–1852)**
   - Potato blight devastates Ireland's staple crop, causing mass starvation and emigration that reduces population by 25%.

7. **Galveston Hurricane (1900)**
   - Deadliest natural disaster in U.S. history kills 6,000–12,000 people, devastating Texas coastal city.

8. **San Francisco Earthquake (1906)**
   - Massive quake and resulting fires destroy most of the city, killing 3,000 and displacing half the population.

9. **Spanish Flu Pandemic (1918–1920)**
   - H1N1 influenza infects 500 million people worldwide, killing 50–100 million in deadliest pandemic in modern history.

10. **Dust Bowl (1930s)**
    - Severe drought and poor farming practices create massive dust storms across American prairies, forcing mass migration.

11. **Great Smog of London (1952)**
    - Thick toxic fog from coal burning kills 12,000 people, leading to landmark clean air legislation.

12. **Mount St. Helens Eruption (1980)**
    - Volcanic explosion in Washington state kills 57, demonstrating modern volcano monitoring capabilities.

13. **Hurricane Katrina (2005)**
    - Category 5 hurricane devastates New Orleans, killing 1,800 and causing $125 billion in damage.

14. **Haiti Earthquake (2010)**
    - Magnitude 7.0 quake near Port-au-Prince kills 220,000–300,000 people in poorest nation in Western Hemisphere.

15. **COVID-19 Pandemic (2019–present)**
    - Novel coronavirus spreads globally, killing millions and triggering unprecedented worldwide lockdowns and economic disruption.
