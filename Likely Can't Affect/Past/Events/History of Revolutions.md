
1. **Athenian Democracy (508 BCE)**
   - Cleisthenes' reforms overthrow tyranny and establish world's first democracy with citizen participation.

2. **English Civil War and Commonwealth (1642–1660)**
   - Parliamentarians execute King Charles I, briefly establishing republic before monarchy's restoration.

3. **Glorious Revolution (1688)**
   - Bloodless overthrow of James II establishes constitutional monarchy and parliamentary supremacy in England.

4. **American Revolution (1765–1783)**
   - Colonial rebellion against British rule creates first modern republic based on Enlightenment principles.

5. **French Revolution (1789–1799)**
   - Overthrow of monarchy unleashes radical social transformation, terror, and Napoleon's rise to power.

6. **Haitian Revolution (1791–1804)**
   - Enslaved people defeat French colonizers in only successful slave revolt, establishing first Black republic.

7. **Latin American Independence (1808–1833)**
   - Bolivar and others liberate Spanish colonies from European rule, creating independent nations.

8. **Greek War of Independence (1821–1829)**
   - Greek revolt against Ottoman Empire succeeds with European support, inspiring nationalist movements.

9. **Revolutions of 1848 (1848–1849)**
   - Wave of liberal and nationalist uprisings sweeps Europe, though most ultimately fail or are suppressed.

10. **Mexican Revolution (1910–1920)**
    - Complex multi-sided conflict overthrows dictatorship, establishing constitutional republic after violent decade.

11. **Russian Revolution (1917)**
    - Bolsheviks overthrow provisional government in October Revolution, establishing world's first communist state.

12. **Cuban Revolution (1953–1959)**
    - Castro's guerrillas overthrow Batista dictatorship, establishing communist state 90 miles from United States.

13. **Iranian Revolution (1979)**
    - Islamic uprising overthrows Shah's Western-backed regime, establishing theocratic republic under Khomeini.

14. **Solidarity Movement (1980–1989)**
    - Polish trade union's peaceful resistance contributes to communism's collapse in Eastern Europe.

15. **Fall of Communism (1989–1991)**
    - Peaceful revolutions sweep Eastern Europe while Soviet Union dissolves, ending Cold War era.

16. **Velvet Revolution (1989)**
    - Czechoslovakia's bloodless transition from communism to democracy led by playwright Václav Havel.

17. **Romanian Revolution (1989)**
    - Violent uprising against Ceaușescu dictatorship ends with dictator's execution and communist regime's fall.

18. **Arab Spring (2010–2012)**
    - Wave of protests and uprisings across Middle East topples dictators but leads to varied outcomes.
