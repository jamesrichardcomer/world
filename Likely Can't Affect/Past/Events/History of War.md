
1. **Trojan War (c. 1200 BCE)**
   - Legendary Greek siege of Troy immortalized in Homer's epics, blending myth and possible historical conflict.

2. **Greco-Persian Wars (499–449 BCE)**
   - Greek city-states repel Persian invasions at Marathon, Thermopylae, and Salamis, preserving Hellenic independence.

3. **Peloponnesian War (431–404 BCE)**
   - Athens versus Sparta in devastating conflict that weakens Greek world and ends Athenian golden age.

4. **Conquests of Alexander the Great (334–323 BCE)**
   - Macedonian king builds empire from Greece to India, spreading Hellenistic culture across known world.

5. **Punic Wars (264–146 BCE)**
   - Rome defeats Carthage in three wars, with Hannibal's Alpine crossing and ultimate destruction of Carthage.

6. **Roman Civil Wars (88–31 BCE)**
   - Series of conflicts including Caesar's crossing of Rubicon, transforming Republic into Empire.

7. **Fall of Rome to Barbarians (376–476 CE)**
   - Germanic invasions culminate in Western Empire's collapse, fragmenting Europe into medieval kingdoms.

8. **Norman Conquest of England (1066)**
   - William the Conqueror's invasion at Hastings permanently alters English culture, language, and governance.

9. **The Crusades (1095–1291)**
   - Christian military campaigns to reclaim Holy Land create lasting tensions between Christianity and Islam.

10. **Hundred Years' War (1337–1453)**
    - England and France clash over succession, featuring Joan of Arc and development of gunpowder warfare.

11. **Fall of Constantinople (1453)**
    - Ottoman Turks capture Byzantine capital, ending Roman Empire and shifting power to Muslim empire.

12. **Spanish Conquest of the Americas (1492–1550s)**
    - European diseases and military technology devastate indigenous civilizations, reshaping New World.

13. **Thirty Years' War (1618–1648)**
    - Devastating religious conflict ravages Central Europe, killing millions and redrawing political map.

14. **English Civil War (1642–1651)**
    - Parliamentarians defeat Royalists, briefly establishing republic and limiting monarchical power.

15. **Seven Years' War (1756–1763)**
    - First global conflict fought across Europe, Americas, and Asia, establishing British imperial dominance.

16. **American Revolutionary War (1775–1783)**
    - Thirteen colonies defeat British Empire, creating United States and inspiring democratic revolutions.

17. **Napoleonic Wars (1803–1815)**
    - French emperor's conquests reshape Europe before defeat at Waterloo establishes new continental order.

18. **American Civil War (1861–1865)**
    - Bloodiest American conflict preserves Union and ends slavery but leaves lasting regional divisions.

19. **World War I (1914–1918)**
    - Industrial-scale slaughter in trenches kills millions, topples empires, and sets stage for future conflicts.

20. **Russian Civil War (1917–1923)**
    - Bolsheviks defeat White forces and foreign intervention, establishing Soviet state.

21. **Spanish Civil War (1936–1939)**
    - Fascist and Republican forces clash in rehearsal for World War II, with international involvement.

22. **World War II (1939–1945)**
    - Deadliest conflict in history defeats Axis powers, reshapes global order, and reveals Holocaust horrors.

23. **Korean War (1950–1953)**
    - Communist North invades South in first major Cold War proxy conflict, ending in stalemate.

24. **Vietnam War (1955–1975)**
    - Communist victory over South Vietnam and U.S. forces reshapes American foreign policy and domestic politics.

25. **Gulf War (1991)**
    - U.S.-led coalition expels Iraq from Kuwait in first major post-Cold War conflict, showcasing military technology.

26. **Yugoslav Wars (1991–2001)**
    - Ethnic conflicts tear apart Yugoslavia in Europe's bloodiest fighting since WWII, including Bosnian genocide.

27. **Iraq War (2003–2011)**
    - U.S.-led invasion topples Saddam Hussein but triggers sectarian violence and regional instability.
