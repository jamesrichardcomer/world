
1. **Human Civilization (300,000 years ago–present)**
   - From stone tools to smartphones, the journey of Homo sapiens across every continent.

2. **Mesopotamia (3500–539 BCE)**
   - The "land between rivers" where civilization began, bringing writing, law codes, and the wheel.

3. **Ancient Egypt (3100–30 BCE)**
   - Pharaohs, pyramids, and a 3,000-year civilization along the Nile that mastered monumental architecture.

4. **Ancient Greece (800–146 BCE)**
   - Democracy, philosophy, and the Olympic Games emerged from city-states that shaped Western thought.

5. **Roman Empire (27 BCE–476 CE)**
   - From a small Italian city to masters of the Mediterranean, Rome built roads, laws, and an empire.

6. **Byzantine Empire (330–1453 CE)**
   - The eastern continuation of Rome preserved classical knowledge for another thousand years from Constantinople.

7. **Medieval Europe (500–1500 CE)**
   - Castles, cathedrals, and feudal lords defined a millennium of European life after Rome's fall.

8. **The Crusades (1095–1291)**
   - Two centuries of religious wars reshaped the relationship between East and West, Christianity and Islam.

9. **The Renaissance (1400–1600)**
   - Art, science, and humanism flourished as Europe rediscovered classical antiquity and looked forward to modernity.

10. **Age of Exploration (1400–1600)**
    - European sailors mapped the world's oceans, connecting continents and reshaping global trade forever.

11. **British Empire (1583–1997)**
    - The largest empire in history spread English language, law, and commerce across a quarter of the globe.

12. **American Revolution (1765–1783)**
    - Thirteen colonies broke from Britain to create a republic built on revolutionary ideals of liberty.

13. **French Revolution (1789–1799)**
    - "Liberty, Equality, Fraternity" toppled a monarchy and unleashed forces that would transform Europe.

14. **Napoleonic Wars (1803–1815)**
    - A Corsican general conquered most of Europe before his final defeat reshaped the continent's borders.

15. **Industrial Revolution (1760–1840)**
    - Steam, steel, and factories transformed agrarian societies into urban industrial powerhouses.

16. **American Civil War (1861–1865)**
    - A nation torn apart over slavery fought its bloodiest war to determine its future.

17. **World War I (1914–1918)**
    - The "war to end all wars" killed millions in trenches and toppled empires across Europe.

18. **Russian Revolution (1917)**
    - Bolsheviks overthrew the Tsar and launched a communist experiment that would last seven decades.

19. **Great Depression (1929–1939)**
    - Economic collapse spread worldwide, bringing mass unemployment and political upheaval.

20. **World War II (1939–1945)**
    - The deadliest conflict in human history defeated fascism but left the world divided between superpowers.

21. **Cold War (1947–1991)**
    - Nuclear-armed superpowers faced off for four decades in a global ideological struggle that never turned hot.

22. **Fall of the Soviet Union (1991)**
    - The communist superpower dissolved, ending the Cold War and reshaping global politics.
