
1. **Controlled Fire (1 million years ago)**
   - Earliest human mastery of energy enables cooking, warmth, protection, and tool-making.

2. **Water Wheels (1st century BCE)**
   - Roman mills harness flowing water for grinding grain, pioneering mechanical power.

3. **Windmills (7th century CE)**
   - Mills capture wind energy for pumping and milling across Europe and Middle East.

4. **Coal Mining (13th century)**
   - Medieval England begins systematic coal extraction as wood becomes scarce.

5. **Steam Engine (1712)**
   - Newcomen's atmospheric engine pumps water from mines, launching Industrial Revolution.

6. **Watt's Improved Steam Engine (1769)**
   - More efficient design makes steam power practical for factories and transportation.

7. **Volta's Battery (1800)**
   - First chemical battery provides portable electricity, enabling new experiments and applications.

8. **Faraday's Electric Generator (1831)**
   - Electromagnetic induction discovery makes generating electricity practical and scalable.

9. **First Oil Well (1859)**
   - Drake's Pennsylvania well launches petroleum industry, initially for kerosene lighting.

10. **Edison's Electric Grid (1882)**
    - Pearl Street Station in New York provides first centralized electric power distribution.

11. **AC Power Transmission (1886)**
    - Westinghouse's alternating current system enables long-distance electricity transmission.

12. **Internal Combustion Engine (1885)**
    - Gasoline engines enable automobiles and later aviation, transforming transportation.

13. **Oil Becomes Dominant Fuel (1900s–1920s)**
    - Automobile and industrial growth make petroleum world's most important energy source.

14. **First Nuclear Power Plant (1951)**
    - EBR-I in Idaho generates electricity from nuclear fission, proving concept's viability.

15. **OPEC Oil Embargo (1973)**
    - Arab oil producers cut supplies to West, triggering energy crisis and efficiency awareness.

16. **Three Mile Island (1979)**
    - Partial nuclear meltdown in Pennsylvania damages public confidence in atomic energy.

17. **Chernobyl Disaster (1986)**
    - Catastrophic Soviet nuclear accident spreads radiation across Europe, intensifying safety concerns.

18. **Wind Power Renaissance (1990s)**
    - Modern wind turbines become economically competitive, especially in Europe.

19. **Solar Cost Revolution (2010s)**
    - Dramatic price drops make solar photovoltaic cheapest electricity source in many regions.

20. **Paris Climate Agreement (2015)**
    - Global accord commits nations to transitioning away from fossil fuels to combat climate change.

21. **Renewable Energy Dominance (2020s)**
    - Solar and wind become cheapest new electricity sources, accelerating clean energy transition.
