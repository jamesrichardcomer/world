
1. **Ancient Greek Natural Philosophy (600–300 BCE)**
   - Thales, Democritus, and Aristotle establishing foundational concepts of matter, motion, and the cosmos through rational inquiry.

2. **Archimedes' Principles (287–212 BCE)**
   - Mathematical physics of levers, buoyancy, and hydrostatics laying groundwork for mechanics and engineering.

3. **Copernican Revolution (1543)**
   - Heliocentric model challenging Earth-centered cosmology and transforming humanity's understanding of its place in the universe.

4. **Galileo's Experiments and Telescopic Observations (1590s–1630s)**
   - Experimental method, laws of motion, and astronomical discoveries establishing modern scientific methodology.

5. **Newton's Principia Mathematica (1687)**
   - Universal gravitation and three laws of motion unifying celestial and terrestrial mechanics in mathematical framework.

6. **Conservation of Energy (1840s–1850s)**
   - Joule, Helmholtz, and others establishing that energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed.

7. **Maxwell's Equations (1865)**
   - Unification of electricity, magnetism, and light as electromagnetic waves revolutionizing physics and technology.

8. **Discovery of the Electron (1897)**
   - J.J. Thomson's identification of the first subatomic particle opening the atomic age.

9. **Planck's Quantum Hypothesis (1900)**
   - Energy quantization solving blackbody radiation problem and initiating quantum revolution.

10. **Einstein's Special Relativity (1905)**
    - Space and time as relative, E=mc², and the constancy of light speed overthrowing Newtonian absolutes.

11. **Einstein's General Relativity (1915)**
    - Gravity as spacetime curvature providing new understanding of cosmos, black holes, and the universe's structure.

12. **Bohr Model and Quantum Atom (1913)**
    - Quantized electron orbits explaining atomic spectra and establishing quantum mechanics' foundation.

13. **Heisenberg's Uncertainty Principle (1927)**
    - Fundamental limit on simultaneously knowing position and momentum revealing probabilistic nature of quantum reality.

14. **Schrödinger Equation (1926)**
    - Wave function formalism providing complete mathematical framework for quantum mechanics.

15. **Discovery of the Neutron (1932)**
    - Chadwick's identification completing the atomic nucleus picture and enabling nuclear physics.

16. **Nuclear Fission Discovery (1938)**
    - Hahn and Strassmann splitting uranium atoms leading to nuclear energy and weapons.

17. **Feynman Diagrams and Quantum Electrodynamics (1940s)**
    - Visual and mathematical tools for calculating particle interactions with unprecedented precision.

18. **Transistor Invention (1947)**
    - Solid-state physics breakthrough revolutionizing electronics and computing.

19. **Discovery of Cosmic Microwave Background (1965)**
    - Penzias and Wilson finding remnant radiation confirming Big Bang theory.

20. **Standard Model Completion (1970s)**
    - Unification of electromagnetic, weak, and strong forces describing all known particles and interactions.

21. **Discovery of Higgs Boson (2012)**
    - CERN confirmation of field giving particles mass, validating Standard Model's final prediction.
