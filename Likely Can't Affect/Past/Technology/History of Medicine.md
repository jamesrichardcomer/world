
1. **Ancient Egyptian Medicine (3000–300 BCE)**
   - Surgical procedures, medicinal plants, and medical papyri documenting systematic approaches to diagnosis and treatment.

2. **Hippocrates and Greek Medicine (460–370 BCE)**
   - Hippocratic Oath, theory of four humors, and the shift from supernatural to natural explanations of disease.

3. **Galen and Roman Medicine (129–216 CE)**
   - Anatomical studies, surgical innovations, and medical theories that dominated Western medicine for over a millennium.

4. **Islamic Golden Age Medicine (800–1300 CE)**
   - Hospitals, pharmacology advances, and preservation of Greek medical texts with major contributions from Avicenna and Al-Razi.

5. **Andreas Vesalius and Modern Anatomy (1543)**
   - Publication of "De Humani Corporis Fabrica" revolutionizing anatomical knowledge through direct observation and dissection.

6. **William Harvey and Circulation (1628)**
   - Discovery that blood circulates through the body pumped by the heart, overturning centuries of Galenic theory.

7. **Germ Theory - Pasteur and Koch (1860s–1880s)**
   - Proof that microorganisms cause disease, transforming understanding of infection and enabling modern hygiene and sterilization.

8. **Anesthesia Revolution (1846)**
   - Introduction of ether anesthesia making painless surgery possible and transforming surgical practice.

9. **Antiseptic Surgery - Joseph Lister (1867)**
   - Application of germ theory to surgery using carbolic acid, dramatically reducing post-operative infections and mortality.

10. **X-Rays Discovery - Wilhelm Röntgen (1895)**
    - Revolutionary imaging technology allowing physicians to see inside the body without surgery.

11. **Psychoanalysis - Sigmund Freud (1890s–1900s)**
    - Development of talk therapy and theories of the unconscious mind founding modern psychiatry and psychology.

12. **Penicillin Discovery - Alexander Fleming (1928)**
    - First true antibiotic ushering in the age of antibiotics and saving millions of lives from bacterial infections.

13. **DNA Double Helix - Watson and Crick (1953)**
    - Discovery of DNA structure enabling understanding of heredity and launching molecular medicine and genetics.

14. **Polio Vaccine - Jonas Salk (1955)**
    - Safe and effective vaccine nearly eradicating a devastating disease and proving the power of immunization.

15. **First Human Heart Transplant - Christiaan Barnard (1967)**
    - Successful organ transplantation demonstrating the possibility of replacing failed organs and advancing immunosuppression.

16. **CT and MRI Scanning (1970s)**
    - Advanced medical imaging providing detailed cross-sectional views of the body revolutionizing diagnosis.

17. **IVF Birth - Louise Brown (1978)**
    - First "test tube baby" opening new possibilities for treating infertility through assisted reproductive technology.

18. **HIV/AIDS Identification (1981–1983)**
    - Recognition and isolation of virus causing global pandemic, leading to new understanding of retroviruses and antiviral therapy.

19. **Human Genome Project Completion (2003)**
    - Complete mapping of human DNA enabling personalized medicine and understanding of genetic diseases.

20. **mRNA Vaccine Technology (2020)**
    - Rapid development of COVID-19 vaccines demonstrating new platform for vaccine creation and global disease response.
