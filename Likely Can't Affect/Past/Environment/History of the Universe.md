
1. **The Big Bang (13.8 billion years ago)**
   - The universe begins from an infinitely hot, dense singularity, rapidly expanding and cooling in the first fractions of a second.

2. **Cosmic Inflation (10⁻³⁶ to 10⁻³² seconds after Big Bang)**
   - Exponential expansion smooths out the universe and creates quantum fluctuations that seed future galaxy formation.

3. **Formation of Fundamental Particles (First seconds)**
   - Quarks combine into protons and neutrons as the universe cools, establishing the basic building blocks of matter.

4. **Nucleosynthesis (First 3 minutes)**
   - Protons and neutrons fuse to form the first atomic nuclei: hydrogen, helium, and trace amounts of lithium.

5. **Recombination and the Cosmic Microwave Background (380,000 years)**
   - Electrons join with nuclei to form neutral atoms, allowing light to travel freely and creating the observable CMB radiation.

6. **The Dark Ages (380,000 to ~200 million years)**
   - Universe filled with neutral hydrogen gas with no stars yet formed, making this era literally dark.

7. **First Stars and Reionization (~200-500 million years)**
   - Massive Population III stars ignite, producing the first light and heavy elements, reionizing the hydrogen gas throughout the universe.

8. **Formation of First Galaxies (~500 million years)**
   - Gravity pulls matter together into the first galaxies, initiating the cosmic web structure we observe today.

9. **Peak Star Formation (10 billion years ago)**
   - The universe reaches maximum rate of star formation, producing most of the stars that exist today.

10. **Formation of the Solar System (4.6 billion years ago)**
    - A collapsing cloud of gas and dust forms our Sun and planets from remnants of earlier stellar generations.

11. **Origin of Life on Earth (~3.8 billion years ago)**
    - First self-replicating molecules emerge in Earth's oceans, beginning the story of biological evolution.

12. **The Great Oxygenation Event (2.4 billion years ago)**
    - Photosynthetic organisms flood Earth's atmosphere with oxygen, fundamentally transforming the planet's chemistry and enabling complex life.

13. **Dark Energy Dominance (5 billion years ago)**
    - The expansion of the universe begins accelerating as dark energy overcomes gravity, determining the cosmos's ultimate fate.

14. **Formation of Modern Sun (4.6 billion years ago to present)**
    - Our Sun enters its stable main sequence phase, providing consistent energy for life on Earth.

15. **The Present Era (Now)**
    - Universe continues expanding and accelerating, galaxies form structures, stars live and die, and conscious observers contemplate cosmic history.
