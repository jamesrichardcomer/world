
1. **Formation of Earth's Atmosphere (4.5–3.8 Billion Years Ago)**
   - Volcanic outgassing created early atmosphere rich in carbon dioxide, water vapor, and nitrogen.

2. **Great Oxidation Event (2.4 Billion Years Ago)**
   - Cyanobacteria produced oxygen through photosynthesis, transforming the atmosphere and causing the first global ice age.

3. **Snowball Earth Episodes (720–635 Million Years Ago)**
   - Planet experienced multiple periods of near-total glaciation before volcanic CO₂ triggered dramatic warming.

4. **Carboniferous Period (359–299 Million Years Ago)**
   - Vast forests buried massive carbon creating coal deposits, while high oxygen levels enabled giant insects.

5. **Permian-Triassic Extinction (252 Million Years Ago)**
   - Massive volcanic eruptions released enormous CO₂, causing extreme warming and ocean acidification.

6. **Dinosaur Era Hothouse (230–66 Million Years Ago)**
   - Much warmer climate than present with no polar ice caps and CO₂ levels 3–6 times current atmosphere.

7. **Paleocene-Eocene Thermal Maximum (56 Million Years Ago)**
   - Rapid carbon release caused 5–8°C warming in thousands of years.

8. **Antarctic Glaciation (34 Million Years Ago)**
   - Falling CO₂ levels allowed permanent ice sheets to form on Antarctica, fundamentally changing global climate.

9. **Pleistocene Ice Ages (2.6 Million–11,700 Years Ago)**
   - Cyclical glacial-interglacial periods driven by orbital variations with massive ice sheets covering continents.

10. **Medieval Warm Period (950–1250 CE)**
    - Regional warming allowed Viking colonization of Greenland and expanded agriculture in Northern Europe.

11. **Little Ice Age (1300–1850 CE)**
    - Cooler temperatures caused crop failures, advancing glaciers, and social upheaval across Europe.

12. **Industrial Revolution (1760–Present)**
    - Fossil fuel combustion began rapidly increasing atmospheric CO₂, initiating human-caused climate alteration.

13. **Rapid Modern Warming (1970s–Present)**
    - Accelerating temperature rise, melting ice sheets, and extreme weather as CO₂ exceeds 420 ppm.
