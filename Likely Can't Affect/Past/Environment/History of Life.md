
1. **Origin of Life (3.8–4.0 billion years ago)**
   - First self-replicating molecules and primitive cells emerge in Earth's primordial oceans, marking the transition from chemistry to biology.

2. **Prokaryotic Cells and Bacteria (3.5 billion years ago)**
   - Simple single-celled organisms without nuclei dominate Earth, establishing basic metabolic processes and beginning to shape the planet.

3. **Photosynthesis and Oxygen Revolution (2.5–3.0 billion years ago)**
   - Cyanobacteria develop photosynthesis, releasing oxygen as waste and fundamentally transforming Earth's atmosphere and chemistry.

4. **Eukaryotic Cells (2.0 billion years ago)**
   - Complex cells with nuclei and organelles emerge through endosymbiosis, enabling greater biological complexity and specialization.

5. **Multicellular Life (1.5 billion years ago)**
   - Cells begin cooperating to form the first multicellular organisms, opening new evolutionary possibilities for size and complexity.

6. **Ediacaran Biota (635–541 million years ago)**
   - Strange, soft-bodied organisms appear in the oceans, representing Earth's first large complex life forms before the Cambrian explosion.

7. **Cambrian Explosion (541 million years ago)**
   - Rapid diversification of animal phyla with hard body parts, including the first arthropods, mollusks, and vertebrate ancestors.

8. **Colonization of Land by Plants (470 million years ago)**
   - Plants transition from water to land, transforming barren continents into habitable environments and altering global climate.

9. **First Land Animals (430 million years ago)**
   - Arthropods and eventually vertebrates follow plants onto land, exploiting new ecological niches and food sources.

10. **Age of Fishes (419–359 million years ago)**
    - Diverse fish species dominate oceans and develop jaws, paired fins, and eventually the skeletal structures that enable land vertebrates.

11. **Tetrapods and Land Vertebrates (375 million years ago)**
    - Fish develop limbs and lungs, transitioning to land and giving rise to amphibians, reptiles, mammals, and birds.

12. **First Forests and Insects (360 million years ago)**
    - Massive tree-ferns create Earth's first forests while insects diversify and some develop flight, the first animals to do so.

13. **Permian-Triassic Extinction (252 million years ago)**
    - The largest mass extinction event eliminates 96% of marine species and 70% of land species, reshaping life's trajectory.

14. **Age of Dinosaurs (230–66 million years ago)**
    - Dinosaurs dominate terrestrial ecosystems while mammals remain small, and birds evolve from theropod dinosaurs.

15. **Flowering Plants Revolution (125 million years ago)**
    - Angiosperms rapidly diversify and transform landscapes, co-evolving with pollinators and becoming dominant plant life.

16. **Cretaceous-Paleogene Extinction (66 million years ago)**
    - Asteroid impact causes mass extinction eliminating non-avian dinosaurs and creating ecological opportunities for mammals.

17. **Age of Mammals (66 million years ago–present)**
    - Mammals rapidly diversify into ecological niches vacated by dinosaurs, evolving into diverse forms from whales to bats to primates.

18. **Primate Evolution (55 million years ago)**
    - Early primates develop in tropical forests, establishing traits like grasping hands, forward-facing eyes, and large brains.

19. **Human Ancestors and Bipedalism (6–7 million years ago)**
    - Hominins split from other apes and develop upright walking, beginning the unique evolutionary path toward modern humans.

20. **Homo Sapiens (300,000 years ago–present)**
    - Anatomically modern humans emerge in Africa with unprecedented cognitive abilities, eventually spreading across the globe and transforming the planet.
