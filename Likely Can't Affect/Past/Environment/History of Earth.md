
1. **Formation of the Earth (4.54 billion years ago)**
   - Solar nebula collapses and Earth forms from accretion of dust and gas, beginning as a molten ball of rock.

2. **The Moon-Forming Impact (4.5 billion years ago)**
   - Massive collision with Mars-sized body Theia ejects debris that coalesces into the Moon, stabilizing Earth's rotation.

3. **Formation of Earth's Oceans (4.4–3.8 billion years ago)**
   - Water vapor condenses as Earth cools, volcanic outgassing and comet impacts contribute to forming the primordial oceans.

4. **Origin of Life (3.8–3.5 billion years ago)**
   - First simple prokaryotic cells emerge in hydrothermal vents or tidal pools, beginning the story of life on Earth.

5. **The Great Oxidation Event (2.4 billion years ago)**
   - Cyanobacteria produce oxygen through photosynthesis, transforming Earth's atmosphere and enabling complex life.

6. **Formation of the Supercontinent Rodinia (1.3–0.9 billion years ago)**
   - Earth's landmasses assemble into a single supercontinent, later fragmenting in cycles that continue today.

7. **Snowball Earth Episodes (720–635 million years ago)**
   - Earth experiences extreme ice ages with glaciers possibly covering the entire planet, followed by rapid warming.

8. **The Cambrian Explosion (541 million years ago)**
   - Rapid diversification of life with most major animal phyla appearing in the fossil record within a few million years.

9. **Colonization of Land by Plants (470 million years ago)**
   - Plants move from water to land, transforming barren continents and creating new ecosystems and oxygen production.

10. **Formation of Pangaea (335–175 million years ago)**
    - All continents merge into the supercontinent Pangaea, profoundly affecting climate, ocean circulation, and species distribution.

11. **The Permian-Triassic Extinction (252 million years ago)**
    - Earth's most severe mass extinction event eliminates 96% of marine species and 70% of terrestrial vertebrates.

12. **Age of Dinosaurs (Mesozoic Era, 252–66 million years ago)**
    - Dinosaurs dominate terrestrial ecosystems while early mammals remain small, and flowering plants emerge.

13. **Cretaceous-Paleogene Extinction (66 million years ago)**
    - Asteroid impact at Chicxulub causes mass extinction ending the dinosaurs and allowing mammals to diversify.

14. **Rise of Mammals and Formation of Modern Continents (66–2.6 million years ago)**
    - Mammals radiate into diverse forms, continents reach near-modern positions, and climate gradually cools.

15. **Quaternary Ice Ages (2.6 million years ago–present)**
    - Cyclical glacial and interglacial periods shape modern geography, with ice sheets advancing and retreating.

16. **Evolution of Homo Sapiens (300,000 years ago)**
    - Modern humans emerge in Africa, eventually migrating across the globe and becoming the dominant species.

17. **Last Glacial Maximum (26,500–19,000 years ago)**
    - Most recent peak of ice age with massive ice sheets covering much of North America and Europe.

18. **Holocene Epoch Begins (11,700 years ago)**
    - Current warm interglacial period begins, enabling agriculture and the rise of human civilization.

19. **The Anthropocene (proposed, mid-20th century–present)**
    - Human activity becomes the dominant influence on Earth's geology and ecosystems, marking a potential new epoch.
