
1. **Electronic Television Invention (1927)**
   - Philo Farnsworth develops electronic image dissector, making modern TV possible.

2. **BBC Television Service Begins (1936)**
   - World's first regular high-definition television service launches in London.

3. **Post-War Television Boom (1946–1955)**
   - TV sets become common in American homes, transforming family life and entertainment.

4. **Golden Age of Television (1950s)**
   - Live anthology dramas, variety shows, and early sitcoms establish television as art form.

5. **I Love Lucy Premieres (1951)**
   - Pioneering sitcom uses three-camera setup and becomes first scripted show filmed before live audience.

6. **Kennedy-Nixon Debates (1960)**
   - First televised presidential debates demonstrate TV's power to shape politics and public opinion.

7. **The Beatles on Ed Sullivan (1964)**
   - 73 million Americans watch performance, demonstrating television's cultural unifying power.

8. **Star Trek Premieres (1966)**
   - Science fiction series becomes cultural phenomenon, pioneering diverse casting and social commentary.

9. **Sesame Street Debuts (1969)**
   - Educational children's program revolutionizes early learning television with research-based approach.

10. **All in the Family (1971)**
    - Norman Lear's sitcom tackles controversial social issues, pushing boundaries of TV content.

11. **VCR Revolution (1970s–1980s)**
    - Home video recording allows time-shifting and collecting, changing how people watch television.

12. **CNN Launches 24-Hour News (1980)**
    - Cable News Network pioneers continuous news coverage, transforming journalism and information flow.

13. **MTV Debuts (1981)**
    - Music television channel revolutionizes music industry and youth culture with constant music videos.

14. **The Simpsons (1989)**
    - Animated sitcom becomes longest-running American primetime series, satirizing American life.

15. **The Sopranos (1999)**
    - HBO drama elevates television storytelling, proving TV can rival cinema in quality and ambition.

16. **TiVo and DVR Technology (1999)**
    - Digital video recorders enable easy recording and ad-skipping, disrupting traditional broadcasting.

17. **Netflix Streaming Dominance (2010s)**
    - Streaming service shifts from DVD rental to original content production, disrupting entire industry.

18. **Game of Thrones (2011)**
    - Epic fantasy series becomes global phenomenon, demonstrating television's cinematic ambitions.

19. **Streaming Wars (2019–present)**
    - Disney+, Apple TV+, HBO Max launch, intensifying competition for subscribers and content.
