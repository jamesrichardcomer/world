
1. **Medieval Sacred Music (500–1400)**
   - Gregorian chant, early polyphony, and the development of musical notation establishing foundations of Western music.

2. **Renaissance Polyphony (1400–1600)**
   - Complex vocal works by Palestrina and Josquin, the rise of secular music, and the beginning of instrumental compositions.

3. **Baroque Era (1600–1750)**
   - Bach, Handel, Vivaldi creating ornate compositions, the birth of opera, and establishment of tonal harmony and orchestral forms.

4. **Classical Period (1750–1820)**
   - Mozart, Haydn, and early Beethoven perfecting symphonic form, sonata structure, and balanced, elegant musical expression.

5. **Romantic Era (1820–1900)**
   - Beethoven, Chopin, Wagner, and Tchaikovsky expanding emotional expression, nationalism in music, and virtuoso performance culture.

6. **Jazz and Blues Emergence (1890s–1920s)**
   - African American musical innovation blending work songs, spirituals, and ragtime into improvisational art forms.

7. **The Swing Era (1930s–1940s)**
   - Big band jazz with Duke Ellington, Count Basie, and Benny Goodman dominating popular music and dance culture.

8. **Birth of Rock and Roll (1950s)**
   - Elvis Presley, Chuck Berry, and Little Richard fusing rhythm and blues with country, creating youth culture revolution.

9. **The Beatles and British Invasion (1960s)**
   - Revolutionary songwriting, studio experimentation, and global pop culture dominance transforming popular music forever.

10. **Motown and Soul Music (1960s)**
    - Detroit's Motown Records and artists like Aretha Franklin bringing Black music to mainstream while maintaining artistic integrity.

11. **Punk Rock (1970s)**
    - Raw, rebellious music from The Ramones, Sex Pistols, and The Clash rejecting rock excess and corporate music industry.

12. **Hip-Hop Revolution (1970s–1980s)**
    - Bronx-born culture of DJing, MCing, and sampling becoming dominant global musical and cultural force.

13. **MTV and Music Video Era (1981–1990s)**
    - Visual presentation becoming integral to music, launching Michael Jackson, Madonna, and changing how music is consumed.

14. **Grunge and Alternative Rock (1990s)**
    - Nirvana, Pearl Jam, and underground rock breaking into mainstream, ending hair metal dominance with authenticity and angst.

15. **Digital Revolution and Streaming (2000s–Present)**
    - Napster, iTunes, and Spotify fundamentally transforming music distribution, discovery, and the economics of the industry.
