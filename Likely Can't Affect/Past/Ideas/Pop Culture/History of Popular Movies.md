
1. **The Great Train Robbery (1903)**
   - Edwin S. Porter's groundbreaking 12-minute Western establishing narrative filmmaking and the grammar of cinema.

2. **The Birth of a Nation (1915)**
   - D.W. Griffith's epic demonstrating film's artistic potential while promoting racist ideology that sparked controversy.

3. **The Jazz Singer (1927)**
   - First feature-length talkie starring Al Jolson, revolutionizing cinema by introducing synchronized dialogue and sound.

4. **Gone with the Wind (1939)**
   - Epic Civil War romance starring Vivien Leigh showcasing Technicolor spectacle and becoming cultural phenomenon.

5. **Citizen Kane (1941)**
   - Orson Welles' masterpiece with innovative deep focus cinematography and non-linear storytelling revolutionizing film technique.

6. **Casablanca (1942)**
   - Humphrey Bogart and Ingrid Bergman's wartime romance defining Hollywood's golden age with memorable dialogue and themes.

7. **The Wizard of Oz (1939)**
   - Judy Garland's journey to Oz pioneering Technicolor fantasy and becoming timeless family entertainment classic.

8. **Psycho (1960)**
   - Alfred Hitchcock's thriller shocking audiences with brutal shower scene and redefining horror genre conventions.

9. **The Godfather (1972)**
   - Francis Ford Coppola's mafia epic starring Marlon Brando establishing crime saga template and modern filmmaking excellence.

10. **Jaws (1975)**
    - Steven Spielberg's shark thriller inventing the summer blockbuster and modern film marketing strategies.

11. **Star Wars (1977)**
    - George Lucas' space opera creating merchandising empire, special effects revolution, and enduring franchise mythology.

12. **E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial (1982)**
    - Spielberg's heartwarming alien story becoming highest-grossing film of the 1980s and defining family entertainment.

13. **Titanic (1997)**
    - James Cameron's disaster romance starring Leonardo DiCaprio and Kate Winslet becoming highest-grossing film of all time.

14. **The Matrix (1999)**
    - Wachowskis' sci-fi action film revolutionizing special effects with bullet time and influencing action cinema aesthetics.

15. **The Lord of the Rings Trilogy (2001–2003)**
    - Peter Jackson's fantasy epic demonstrating CGI potential and proving trilogy storytelling viable for modern audiences.

16. **Avatar (2009)**
    - James Cameron's 3D spectacle becoming highest-grossing film ever and pushing boundaries of motion-capture technology.

17. **Avengers: Endgame (2019)**
    - Marvel's culminating superhero team-up becoming second highest-grossing film and validating interconnected cinematic universe model.
