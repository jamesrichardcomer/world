
1. **Ancient Number Systems (3000 BCE)**
   - Sumerians and Egyptians develop numeral systems for practical calculations and record-keeping.

2. **Babylonian Mathematics (2000–500 BCE)**
   - Advanced arithmetic, algebra, and geometry including quadratic equations and Pythagorean theorem anticipation.

3. **Euclid's Elements (c. 300 BCE)**
   - Systematic geometry treatise establishing axiomatic method that dominates mathematics for two millennia.

4. **Archimedes' Discoveries (287–212 BCE)**
   - Greek mathematician calculates pi, discovers principles of calculus, and pioneers mathematical physics.

5. **Hindu-Arabic Numerals (500–700 CE)**
   - Indian mathematicians develop decimal place-value system with zero, revolutionizing calculation.

6. **Fibonacci Sequence (1202)**
   - Italian mathematician introduces Hindu-Arabic numerals to Europe and discovers famous number sequence.

7. **Perspective in Renaissance Art (1400s)**
   - Mathematical principles of perspective transform painting while advancing projective geometry.

8. **Cartesian Coordinates (1637)**
   - Descartes links algebra and geometry, enabling graphical representation of equations.

9. **Pascal's Triangle and Probability (1653)**
   - French mathematician develops combinatorics and lays foundations of probability theory with Fermat.

10. **Newton's Calculus (1665–1666)**
    - English physicist develops differential and integral calculus for analyzing motion and change.

11. **Leibniz's Calculus (1675–1676)**
    - German philosopher independently invents calculus with superior notation still used today.

12. **Euler's Contributions (1730s–1780s)**
    - Swiss genius revolutionizes multiple fields including graph theory, complex analysis, and number theory.

13. **Gauss's Number Theory (1801)**
    - German mathematician's Disquisitiones Arithmeticae transforms number theory into rigorous science.

14. **Non-Euclidean Geometry (1823–1830s)**
    - Bolyai and Lobachevsky independently discover geometries where parallel postulate doesn't hold.

15. **Boolean Algebra (1854)**
    - George Boole creates algebraic logic system that later becomes foundation for computer science.

16. **Cantor's Set Theory (1874–1884)**
    - German mathematician proves infinite sets have different sizes, founding modern set theory.

17. **Gödel's Incompleteness Theorems (1931)**
    - Austrian logician proves any consistent mathematical system has unprovable true statements.

18. **Turing Machine (1936)**
    - British mathematician defines theoretical computer, establishing limits of computation.

19. **Four Color Theorem Proof (1976)**
    - First major theorem proved using computer assistance, sparking debate about mathematical proof.

20. **Fermat's Last Theorem Proved (1995)**
    - Andrew Wiles solves 358-year-old problem using advanced modern mathematics.
