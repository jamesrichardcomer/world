
1. **Code of Hammurabi (c. 1754 BCE)**
   - One of the earliest written legal codes establishing proportional justice and the principle of "an eye for an eye."

2. **Roman Law and the Twelve Tables (450 BCE)**
   - Foundation of Roman legal system codifying rights and procedures that influenced all subsequent Western law.

3. **Corpus Juris Civilis (529–534 CE)**
   - Justinian's comprehensive codification of Roman law that became the basis for civil law systems across Europe.

4. **Magna Carta (1215)**
   - English charter limiting royal power and establishing that even kings are subject to law, foundational to constitutional government.

5. **Canon Law Development (12th–13th centuries)**
   - Systematic ecclesiastical law governing the Catholic Church that influenced marriage, contracts, and procedural law.

6. **Common Law Emergence (12th–13th centuries)**
   - English system of judge-made precedent and jury trials creating flexible, case-based legal reasoning.

7. **Habeas Corpus Act (1679)**
   - English law protecting against unlawful detention and ensuring judicial review of imprisonment.

8. **Montesquieu's Spirit of the Laws (1748)**
   - Influential treatise advocating separation of powers and checks and balances in government.

9. **U.S. Constitution and Bill of Rights (1787–1791)**
   - Written constitution establishing federalism, separation of powers, and fundamental individual rights.

10. **Napoleonic Code (1804)**
    - French civil code emphasizing clarity, accessibility, and equality before the law that influenced legal systems worldwide.

11. **Abolition of Slavery (19th century)**
    - Legal elimination of slavery in the British Empire (1833), United States (1865), and globally transforming human rights law.

12. **Married Women's Property Acts (1870s–1880s)**
    - Laws granting women independent property rights and legal personhood separate from their husbands.

13. **Nuremberg Trials (1945–1946)**
    - International prosecution of Nazi war criminals establishing principles of crimes against humanity and individual accountability.

14. **Universal Declaration of Human Rights (1948)**
    - UN proclamation of fundamental human rights establishing international standards for dignity and freedom.

15. **Brown v. Board of Education (1954)**
    - U.S. Supreme Court decision declaring racial segregation unconstitutional and launching the civil rights legal revolution.

16. **European Convention on Human Rights (1953)**
    - International treaty protecting civil and political rights enforced by the European Court of Human Rights.

17. **Civil Rights Act (1964)**
    - Landmark U.S. legislation prohibiting discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex, or national origin.

18. **Miranda v. Arizona (1966)**
    - U.S. Supreme Court ruling requiring police to inform suspects of their rights, protecting against self-incrimination.

19. **Roe v. Wade (1973)**
    - U.S. Supreme Court decision establishing constitutional right to abortion, later overturned in Dobbs v. Jackson (2022).

20. **International Criminal Court Established (2002)**
    - Permanent tribunal to prosecute individuals for genocide, crimes against humanity, and war crimes globally.
