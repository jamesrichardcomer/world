
1. **Ancient Greek Schools (387 BCE)**
   - Plato and Aristotle establish philosophical schools emphasizing dialectic and scientific inquiry.

2. **Medieval Universities (1088 onwards)**
   - Bologna, Oxford, and Paris establish model of university education with set curricula and degrees.

3. **Printing Press Revolution (1440s)**
   - Gutenberg's invention democratizes access to books, transforming literacy and education.

4. **Jesuit Education System (1540s)**
   - Catholic order establishes rigorous classical education model spreading globally.

5. **Enlightenment Educational Theory (1700s)**
   - Rousseau and Locke develop influential ideas about childhood development and learning.

6. **Prussian Education System (1763)**
   - Compulsory state education model later adopted worldwide establishes standardized schooling.

7. **Common School Movement (1830s–1850s)**
   - Horace Mann champions free public education in America, establishing state school systems.

8. **Kindergarten Movement (1837)**
   - Friedrich Fröbel creates educational approach focused on play and child development.

9. **Morrill Land-Grant Act (1862)**
   - U.S. law establishes agricultural and technical colleges, democratizing higher education.

10. **Progressive Education (1890s–1950s)**
    - John Dewey advocates experiential learning and education for democracy over rote memorization.

11. **Montessori Method (1907)**
    - Maria Montessori develops child-centered approach emphasizing independence and hands-on learning.

12. **GI Bill (1944)**
    - American law provides college education for millions of veterans, transforming access to higher education.

13. **Brown v. Board of Education (1954)**
    - Supreme Court ruling desegregates American schools, though implementation proves slow and contested.

14. **Special Education Rights (1975)**
    - U.S. law guarantees free appropriate education for children with disabilities.

15. **Online Learning Platforms (2000s)**
    - Khan Academy, Coursera, and MOOCs democratize access to educational content globally.

16. **Pandemic Remote Learning (2020)**
    - COVID-19 forces massive shift to online education, revealing digital divide and accelerating innovation.
