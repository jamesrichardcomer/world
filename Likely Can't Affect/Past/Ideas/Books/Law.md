
1. **The Code of Hammurabi — (c. 1754 BCE)**
   - Ancient Babylonian law code establishing retributive justice principles and regulated social relations through written rules.

2. **The Laws — Plato (c. 360–347 BCE)**
   - Dialogue outlining an ideal legal system grounded in reason and virtue to guide citizens toward the good life.

3. **De Legibus (On the Laws) — Cicero (c. 52 BCE)**
   - Philosophical treatise arguing that true law derives from natural reason and applies universally to all people.

4. **Corpus Juris Civilis (Body of Civil Law) — Justinian I (529–534 CE)**
   - Monumental codification of Roman law that became the foundation of civil law systems worldwide.

5. **Commentaries on the Laws of England — William Blackstone (1765–1769)**
   - Comprehensive systematization of English common law that shaped legal education and practice across the English-speaking world.

6. **An Introduction to the Principles of Morals and Legislation — Jeremy Bentham (1789)**
   - Utilitarian framework proposing that laws should maximize happiness and minimize suffering for the greatest number.

7. **Ancient Law — Henry Maine (1861)**
   - Historical study tracing legal evolution from status-based societies to contract-based modern systems.

8. **The Common Law — Oliver Wendell Holmes Jr. (1881)**
   - Examination of common law's development through experience rather than logic, emphasizing law's practical evolution.

9. **The Path of the Law — Oliver Wendell Holmes Jr. (1897)**
   - Influential essay defining law pragmatically as predictions of what courts will do, not abstract moral principles.

10. **The Nature of the Judicial Process — Benjamin N. Cardozo (1921)**
    - Candid exploration of how judges actually decide cases, balancing precedent, policy, and social context.

11. **Pure Theory of Law — Hans Kelsen (1934)**
    - Positivist theory separating law from morality and grounding legal validity in hierarchical norms rather than natural law.

12. **The Concept of Law — H.L.A. Hart (1961)**
    - Analytical philosophy masterwork defining law as a system of primary and secondary rules recognized by officials.

13. **Taking Rights Seriously — Ronald Dworkin (1977)**
    - Critique of legal positivism arguing that individual rights constrain government and judges must interpret law through principles.

14. **Law's Empire — Ronald Dworkin (1986)**
    - Theory of law as interpretive practice requiring judges to construct the best moral justification of legal materials.

15. **Rights Talk — Mary Ann Glendon (1991)**
    - Critique of American rights discourse for being absolutist and individualistic while neglecting responsibilities and community.

16. **A Matter of Interpretation: Federal Courts and the Law — Antonin Scalia (1997)**
    - Defense of textualism and originalism in constitutional and statutory interpretation against purposivist approaches.

17. **The Rule of Law — Tom Bingham (2010)**
    - Clear articulation of the rule of law's meaning and importance as the foundation of democratic society.

18. **Reading Law: The Interpretation of Legal Texts — Antonin Scalia & Bryan A. Garner (2012)**
    - Comprehensive guide to textualist interpretation methods with canons for reading statutes and constitutions.
