
1. **The Hebrew Bible / Old Testament — Various (c. 1200–100 BCE)**
   - Sacred scriptures of Judaism comprising the Torah, Prophets, and Writings that narrate creation, covenant, and Israel's relationship with God.

2. **I Ching (Book of Changes) — Various (compiled c. 9th–3rd century BCE)**
   - Ancient Chinese divination text presenting 64 hexagrams used to understand cosmic patterns and guide decision-making.

3. **Bhagavad Gita — Attributed to Vyasa (c. 2nd century BCE–2nd century CE)**
   - Hindu philosophical dialogue where Krishna teaches Arjuna about duty, devotion, and paths to spiritual liberation on the battlefield.

4. **The New Testament — Various (c. 50–120 CE)**
   - Christian scriptures documenting Jesus's life, teachings, death, and resurrection, plus the early church's formation and theology.

5. **Mishnah — Various (c. 200 CE)**
   - First major written collection of Jewish oral law organizing rabbinic debates and legal rulings into six orders.

6. **The Babylonian Talmud — Various (compiled c. 5th–6th century CE)**
   - Monumental compilation of rabbinic discussions interpreting the Mishnah, serving as the central text of Jewish law and tradition.

7. **Rule of St. Benedict — Benedict of Nursia (c. 530)**
   - Monastic guide prescribing daily rhythms of prayer, work, and communal living that shaped Western monasticism.

8. **Qur'an — Attributed to Muhammad (compiled c. 7th century)**
   - Islam's holy book revealed to Muhammad containing God's final guidance on faith, law, and righteous living.

9. **95 Theses — Martin Luther (1517)**
   - Controversial propositions challenging Catholic indulgences that sparked the Protestant Reformation.

10. **Institutes of the Christian Religion — John Calvin (1536–1559)**
    - Systematic exposition of Reformed Protestant theology emphasizing God's sovereignty, predestination, and scripture's authority.

11. **The Book of Mormon — Joseph Smith (1830)**
    - Latter-day Saint scripture recounting ancient American civilizations visited by Jesus Christ after his resurrection.
