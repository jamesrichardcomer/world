
1. **The Wealth of Nations — Adam Smith (1776)**
   - Foundational text of modern economics arguing that free markets and the "invisible hand" of self-interest drive prosperity.

2. **On the Principles of Political Economy and Taxation — David Ricardo (1817)**
   - Systematic analysis of value, rent, and comparative advantage that shaped classical economic theory.

3. **Capital, Volume I — Karl Marx (1867)**
   - Critique of capitalism revealing how labor exploitation and surplus value drive capitalist accumulation.

4. **The Theory of the Leisure Class — Thorstein Veblen (1899)**
   - Satirical analysis of conspicuous consumption and how the wealthy use wasteful spending to signal status.

5. **The General Theory of Employment, Interest and Money — John Maynard Keynes (1936)**
   - Revolutionary work arguing that government intervention is necessary to manage demand and prevent economic downturns.

6. **The Road to Serfdom — F.A. Hayek (1944)**
   - Warning that central economic planning inevitably leads to totalitarianism and loss of individual freedom.

7. **Theory of Games and Economic Behavior — John von Neumann & Oskar Morgenstern (1944)**
   - Mathematical framework for analyzing strategic decision-making that founded modern game theory.

8. **The Strategy of Conflict — Thomas Schelling (1960)**
   - Analysis of bargaining and deterrence showing how credible commitments and strategic irrationality shape outcomes.

9. **Capitalism and Freedom — Milton Friedman (1962)**
   - Passionate defense of free markets and limited government as foundations of both economic prosperity and political liberty.

10. **Free to Choose — Milton & Rose Friedman (1980)**
    - Accessible argument for economic freedom demonstrating how markets outperform government planning across sectors.

11. **The Evolution of Cooperation — Robert Axelrod (1984)**
    - Game-theoretic exploration of how cooperative behavior emerges and persists through repeated interactions.

12. **Freakonomics — Steven Levitt & Stephen Dubner (2005)**
    - Provocative application of economic thinking to unexpected topics from sumo wrestling to parenting.

13. **The Black Swan — Nassim Nicholas Taleb (2007)**
    - Examination of rare, unpredictable events that have massive impact and our failure to account for them.

14. **Predictably Irrational — Dan Ariely (2008)**
    - Behavioral economics exploration of the systematic ways humans deviate from rational decision-making.

15. **The Big Short — Michael Lewis (2010)**
    - Gripping account of the traders who predicted and profited from the 2008 financial crisis.

16. **The Bed of Procrustes — Nassim Nicholas Taleb (2010)**
    - Collection of aphorisms exposing how we distort reality to fit our theories and models.

17. **Debt: The First 5,000 Years — David Graeber (2011)**
    - Anthropological history arguing that debt and credit preceded money and shaped human morality and society.

18. **Thinking, Fast and Slow — Daniel Kahneman (2011)**
    - Synthesis of decades of research on the two systems of thinking that drive judgment and decision-making.

19. **Antifragile — Nassim Nicholas Taleb (2012)**
    - Framework for understanding systems that gain strength from disorder, volatility, and stress.

20. **Skin in the Game — Nassim Nicholas Taleb (2017)**
    - Argument that ethical behavior and sound decision-making require bearing the consequences of one's actions.
