
1. **Émile, or On Education — Jean-Jacques Rousseau (1762)**
   - Philosophical treatise arguing that children should develop naturally through experience rather than formal instruction.

2. **The Subjection of Women — John Stuart Mill (1869)**
   - Pioneering feminist argument that the legal subordination of women is unjust and harms society's progress.

3. **The Souls of Black Folk — W.E.B. Du Bois (1903)**
   - Landmark work exploring African American consciousness, "double consciousness," and the color line in American society.

4. **The Protestant Ethic and the Spirit of Capitalism — Max Weber (1905)**
   - Sociological analysis linking Protestant values of hard work and thrift to the rise of modern capitalism.

5. **The Second Sex — Simone de Beauvoir (1949)**
   - Existentialist feminist text arguing that women are made, not born, and have been historically defined as "the Other."

6. **The Death and Life of Great American Cities — Jane Jacobs (1961)**
   - Urban planning critique championing mixed-use neighborhoods and street-level vitality over top-down modernist design.
