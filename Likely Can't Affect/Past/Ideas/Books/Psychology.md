
1. **The Principles of Psychology — William James (1890)**
   - Monumental two-volume work establishing psychology as a science while exploring consciousness, habit, emotion, and the stream of thought.

2. **The Interpretation of Dreams — Sigmund Freud (1900)**
   - Foundational psychoanalytic text arguing that dreams are wish fulfillments revealing unconscious desires through symbolic interpretation.

3. **Three Essays on the Theory of Sexuality — Sigmund Freud (1905)**
   - Revolutionary work proposing that sexuality develops through childhood stages and that sexual perversions are variations rather than diseases.

4. **Psychological Types — Carl Jung (1921)**
   - Introduction of introversion and extraversion along with four cognitive functions, forming the basis for modern personality typology.

5. **Conditioned Reflexes — Ivan Pavlov (1927)**
   - Detailed account of classical conditioning experiments showing how neutral stimuli can trigger learned physiological responses.

6. **Civilization and Its Discontents — Sigmund Freud (1930)**
   - Exploration of the inevitable conflict between individual instincts and the demands of civilized society, leading to widespread discontent.

7. **Modern Man in Search of a Soul — Carl Jung (1933)**
   - Collection of essays exploring the spiritual crisis of modern life and the journey toward psychological wholeness and meaning.

8. **Man's Search for Meaning — Viktor Frankl (1946)**
   - Holocaust survivor's account demonstrating that finding meaning and purpose is essential to psychological survival even in extreme suffering.

9. **The Psychology of Intelligence — Jean Piaget (1947)**
   - Systematic theory describing how children's cognitive abilities develop through distinct stages from infancy to adolescence.

10. **Childhood and Society — Erik Erikson (1950)**
    - Introduction of the eight-stage theory of psychosocial development spanning from infancy to old age.

11. **Science and Human Behavior — B.F. Skinner (1953)**
    - Comprehensive application of behaviorist principles arguing that all behavior is shaped by environmental reinforcement and consequences.

12. **Motivation and Personality — Abraham Maslow (1954)**
    - Presentation of the hierarchy of needs culminating in self-actualization, shifting psychology's focus toward human potential.

13. **On Becoming a Person — Carl Rogers (1961)**
    - Humanistic psychologist's exploration of client-centered therapy emphasizing unconditional positive regard and the actualizing tendency.

14. **Obedience to Authority — Stanley Milgram (1974)**
    - Disturbing experiments revealing that ordinary people will inflict harm on others when instructed by legitimate authority figures.

15. **The Origin of Consciousness in the Breakdown of the Bicameral Mind — Julian Jaynes (1976)**
    - Controversial theory proposing that ancient humans lacked consciousness and experienced their own thoughts as commands from gods.

16. **Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion — Robert Cialdini (1984)**
    - Identification of six universal principles of influence and compliance used in marketing, sales, and everyday social interactions.

17. **The Blank Slate — Steven Pinker (2002)**
    - Argument against the idea that human nature is infinitely malleable, demonstrating the role of innate faculties and evolutionary psychology.

18. **The Righteous Mind — Jonathan Haidt (2012)**
    - Exploration of moral psychology showing that intuition drives moral judgments while reasoning serves to justify pre-existing beliefs.
