
1. **Ancient Society — Lewis Henry Morgan (1877)**
   - Foundational text proposing that all societies evolve through stages from savagery to barbarism to civilization.

2. **Primitive Culture — Edward Burnett Tylor (1871)**
   - Early anthropological work that defined culture as a complex whole and introduced the concept of cultural evolution.

3. **The Mind of Primitive Man — Franz Boas (1911)**
   - Groundbreaking critique of racial determinism arguing that cultural differences arise from historical circumstances, not biological race.

4. **Race, Language, and Culture — Franz Boas (1940)**
   - Collection of essays dismantling scientific racism by demonstrating that race, language, and culture vary independently.

5. **Structure and Function in Primitive Society — A.R. Radcliffe-Brown (1952)**
   - Structural-functionalist analysis examining how social institutions maintain stability and cohesion in traditional societies.

6. **Structural Anthropology — Claude Lévi-Strauss (1958)**
   - Application of linguistic structuralism to culture, revealing universal patterns underlying myths, kinship, and symbolism.

7. **The Interpretation of Cultures — Clifford Geertz (1973)**
   - Seminal work advocating "thick description" and treating culture as symbolic systems requiring interpretive analysis.

8. **The Mismeasure of Man — Stephen Jay Gould (1981)**
   - Critique of biological determinism exposing the flawed science behind IQ testing and racial hierarchies.
