
1. **Origin of Life (3.8–3.5 Billion Years Ago)**
   - First single-celled organisms emerge in Earth's oceans, marking the beginning of biological evolution.

2. **Photosynthesis and Oxygen Revolution (2.4 Billion Years Ago)**
   - Cyanobacteria develop photosynthesis, releasing oxygen and transforming Earth's atmosphere and enabling complex life.

3. **Eukaryotic Cells (2.1–1.6 Billion Years Ago)**
   - Complex cells with nuclei evolve through endosymbiosis, creating the foundation for all multicellular life.

4. **Multicellular Life (600 Million Years Ago)**
   - First multicellular organisms appear, leading to explosion of diverse body plans and complexity.

5. **Cambrian Explosion (541 Million Years Ago)**
   - Rapid diversification of animal phyla with hard body parts, eyes, and predator-prey relationships emerging.

6. **Colonization of Land (475–360 Million Years Ago)**
   - Plants, fungi, and arthropods move onto land, followed by vertebrates, transforming terrestrial ecosystems.

7. **Age of Dinosaurs (252–66 Million Years Ago)**
   - Dinosaurs dominate terrestrial ecosystems while mammals remain small, and birds evolve from theropod dinosaurs.

8. **Mass Extinction Event (66 Million Years Ago)**
   - Asteroid impact causes extinction of non-avian dinosaurs, opening ecological niches for mammalian radiation.

9. **Rise of Mammals (66–23 Million Years Ago)**
   - Mammals diversify rapidly into numerous forms, including primates, whales, bats, and large herbivores.

10. **Human Evolution Begins (7–6 Million Years Ago)**
    - Hominins split from chimpanzee lineage in Africa, beginning the unique evolutionary path toward modern humans.

11. **Bipedalism (4.2 Million Years Ago)**
    - Australopithecines walk upright, freeing hands for tool use and fundamentally changing hominin evolution.

12. **Homo Genus Emerges (2.8 Million Years Ago)**
    - First members of genus Homo appear with larger brains and more sophisticated stone tools.

13. **Control of Fire (1 Million Years Ago)**
    - Homo erectus harnesses fire for cooking, protection, and warmth, enabling brain growth and social development.

14. **Homo sapiens Evolution (300,000 Years Ago)**
    - Anatomically modern humans emerge in Africa with advanced cognition, language, and symbolic thinking.

15. **Out of Africa Migration (70,000–60,000 Years Ago)**
    - Modern humans migrate from Africa to colonize the entire world, encountering and sometimes interbreeding with other hominins.

16. **Agricultural Revolution (12,000 Years Ago)**
    - Domestication of plants and animals transforms human societies and creates new evolutionary pressures on both humans and domesticates.

17. **Charles Darwin's On the Origin of Species (1859)**
    - Publication of theory of evolution by natural selection providing scientific framework for understanding life's diversity.

18. **Rediscovery of Mendel's Laws (1900)**
    - Genetic inheritance principles rediscovered, eventually reconciling with Darwin's theory to form modern evolutionary synthesis.

19. **Discovery of DNA Structure (1953)**
    - Watson and Crick reveal DNA's double helix, providing molecular basis for heredity and evolution.

20. **Neutral Theory of Molecular Evolution (1968)**
    - Motoo Kimura proposes that most genetic changes are neutral, revolutionizing understanding of molecular evolution and genetic drift.
