## The Major Religions
### Christianity (~2.4 billion)
- **Core belief:** One God; Jesus Christ is the Son of God who died and rose again for humanity's salvation
- **Text:** The Bible (Old and New Testaments)
- **Major branches:**
  - Catholic (~1.27 billion) — Pope as head; centralized authority; Rome
  - Protestant (~625 million) — Many denominations (Baptist, Methodist, Lutheran, Pentecostal, etc.); emphasis on scripture alone; no single authority
  - Eastern Orthodox (~295 million) — Patriarch-led; strong in Russia, Greece, Eastern Europe
- **Where:** Americas, Europe, sub-Saharan Africa, Oceania. Declining in Western Europe/North America; growing rapidly in Africa and Asia
- **Trend:** 69% of all Christians now live in the Global South, projected to reach 78% by 2050

### Islam (~2.0 billion)
- **Core belief:** One God (Allah); Muhammad is the final prophet; submission to God's will
- **Text:** Quran; Hadith (sayings/practices of Muhammad)
- **Five Pillars:** Shahada (declaration of faith), Salat (prayer 5x/day), Zakat (charity), Sawm (fasting during Ramadan), Hajj (pilgrimage to Mecca)
- **Major branches:**
  - Sunni (~85–90%) — Majority; follows elected caliphs after Muhammad
  - Shia (~10–15%) — Follows Ali (Muhammad's son-in-law) as rightful successor; concentrated in Iran, Iraq, parts of Lebanon
- **Where:** Middle East, North Africa, South/Southeast Asia, growing in Sub-Saharan Africa
- **Trend:** Fastest-growing religion worldwide, primarily through high birth rates, not conversion

### Hinduism (~1.2 billion)
- **Core belief:** Brahman (universal soul/reality); many gods as manifestations; karma, dharma, reincarnation, moksha (liberation from the cycle of rebirth)
- **Texts:** Vedas, Upanishads, Bhagavad Gita, epics (Mahabharata, Ramayana)
- **No single founder, no single authority, no single creed** — more a family of traditions
- **Where:** Predominantly India (~94% of Hindus); Nepal; diaspora communities worldwide
- **Trend:** Growing with India's population; not a proselytizing religion

### Buddhism (~324 million)
- **Core belief:** Life involves suffering (dukkha); suffering arises from attachment/desire; the path to ending suffering is the Eightfold Path; no creator god
- **Founded by:** Siddhartha Gautama (the Buddha), ~500 BCE
- **Major branches:**
  - Theravada — Southeast Asia (Thailand, Myanmar, Sri Lanka, Cambodia)
  - Mahayana — East Asia (China, Japan, Korea, Vietnam)
  - Vajrayana/Tibetan — Tibet, Mongolia, Bhutan
- **Trend:** Only major religion that shrank in absolute numbers 2010–2020 (lost 19 million adherents). Declining in East Asia due to secularization, but growing interest in the West.

### Judaism (~15 million)
- **Core belief:** One God; covenant between God and the Jewish people; Torah as central text; emphasis on law, ethics, and study
- **Branches:** Orthodox, Conservative, Reform
- **Where:** Israel (~7 million, 45.9% of world's Jews), United States (~6 million)
- **Trend:** Slow growth (~6% over 2010–2020). Not a proselytizing religion. Would likely be twice as large without the Holocaust.

## Religiously Unaffiliated (~1.9 billion)
Includes atheists, agnostics, and "nothing in particular." Third-largest group globally.

- 67% live in China
- Growing rapidly in North America (+13 percentage points from 2010–2020, now ~30%)
- Growing in Europe (now ~25%)
- Majority in Netherlands, Uruguay, New Zealand, Czech Republic, Japan, China, and others
- At a "demographic disadvantage" — older populations, lower fertility rates (~1.6 children per woman)
- Despite growing as % of Western populations, shrinking as % of world population due to low birth rates

## Geographic Distribution
- **Christianity dominant:** Americas, Europe, sub-Saharan Africa, Oceania
- **Islam dominant:** Middle East, North Africa, Central/South/Southeast Asia
- **Hinduism dominant:** India, Nepal
- **Buddhism dominant:** Southeast Asia, East Asia, Tibet
- **Judaism concentrated:** Israel, United States
- **Unaffiliated concentrated:** East Asia (especially China), Western Europe, Oceania, North America
- **Folk religions:** Sub-Saharan Africa, China, indigenous populations worldwide

---

## Key Trends
- Islam and Christianity together account for over half the world's population
- Islam is growing fastest (high birth rates, young populations); projected to nearly equal Christianity by 2050
- Christianity shifting from Global North to Global South
- Secularization accelerating in Western Europe, North America, Oceania, and East Asia
- Religious fertility advantage: Muslims (~2.9 children/woman), Christians (~2.6), Hindus (~2.3), Buddhists (~1.6), Unaffiliated (~1.6)
- Countries are becoming more religiously polarized — very religious or very secular, with less middle ground
