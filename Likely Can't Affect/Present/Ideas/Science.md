## Physics
### The Standard Model
The best current theory of fundamental particles and forces. Describes 17 particles:

**Matter particles (fermions):**
- **Quarks** (6 types) — Make up protons and neutrons. Come in "up," "down," "charm," "strange," "top," "bottom."
- **Leptons** (6 types) — Includes electrons, muons, taus, and their corresponding neutrinos.

**Force-carrying particles (bosons):**
- **Photon** — Carries electromagnetic force (light, electricity, magnetism)
- **Gluon** — Carries strong nuclear force (holds quarks together inside protons/neutrons)
- **W and Z bosons** — Carry weak nuclear force (radioactive decay)
- **Higgs boson** — Gives particles mass (discovered 2012)

**What's missing:** Gravity. The Standard Model does not include gravity. General relativity describes gravity well at large scales, but no one has successfully unified it with quantum mechanics.

### The Four Fundamental Forces
| Force | Relative Strength | Range | What It Does |
|---|---|---|---|
| Strong nuclear | 1 (strongest) | ~10⁻¹⁵ m | Holds quarks together; holds atomic nuclei together |
| Electromagnetic | 1/137 | Infinite | Light, electricity, magnetism, chemistry, holds atoms together |
| Weak nuclear | 10⁻⁶ | ~10⁻¹⁸ m | Radioactive decay; nuclear fusion in stars |
| Gravity | 10⁻³⁹ (weakest) | Infinite | Attracts all mass/energy; shapes planets, stars, galaxies |

### Relativity (Einstein)
- **Special relativity** — Speed of light is constant (~300,000 km/s) for all observers. Nothing can travel faster. Time slows and mass increases as you approach light speed. E = mc² (mass and energy are interchangeable).
- **General relativity** — Gravity is the curvature of spacetime caused by mass/energy. Massive objects bend space and time. Confirmed by GPS corrections, gravitational lensing, and detection of gravitational waves (2015).

### Quantum Mechanics
- At atomic/subatomic scales, particles behave as both waves and particles.
- **Uncertainty principle** — You can't precisely know both a particle's position and momentum simultaneously.
- **Superposition** — Particles exist in multiple states until measured.
- **Entanglement** — Two particles can be linked so measuring one instantly affects the other, regardless of distance.
- Quantum mechanics is extremely well-tested and underlies all modern electronics, lasers, and chemistry.

### Thermodynamics
1. Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed.
2. Entropy (disorder) always increases in a closed system. Things naturally move from order to disorder.
3. Absolute zero (0 Kelvin / -273.15°C) can never be reached.

### What We Don't Know
- **Dark matter** (~27% of the universe) — Exerts gravity but doesn't interact with light. Never directly detected.
- **Dark energy** (~68% of the universe) — Driving the accelerating expansion of the universe. Completely mysterious.
- **Ordinary matter** — Only ~5% of the universe.
- **Quantum gravity** — How to reconcile quantum mechanics with general relativity.
- **Why is there something rather than nothing?** — Why does matter exist at all? (Matter-antimatter asymmetry.)

## Chemistry (basics)
- Everything is made of ~118 elements (organized in the periodic table).
- Atoms bond to form molecules through electromagnetic interactions between their electrons.
- Most common elements in the universe: hydrogen (~75%), helium (~24%), everything else (~1%).
- Most common elements in the human body: oxygen (65%), carbon (18%), hydrogen (10%), nitrogen (3%).
- Chemical reactions rearrange atoms; nuclear reactions change atoms themselves.

## Biology
### How Life Works
- All known life is based on **DNA** — a molecule that stores genetic instructions using a 4-letter code (A, T, C, G).
- DNA → RNA → Protein (the "central dogma"). Proteins do almost everything in cells.
- **Cells** are the basic unit of life. Humans have ~37 trillion cells.
- **Two types:** prokaryotes (bacteria, archaea — no nucleus) and eukaryotes (plants, animals, fungi — have a nucleus).

### Evolution
- All life shares a common ancestor (~3.8 billion years ago).
- Evolution works through: random genetic mutation → natural selection (organisms better adapted to their environment survive and reproduce more).
- Evolution is not goal-directed. It doesn't aim for "better" — just better-fitted to current conditions.
- Humans share ~98.7% of DNA with chimpanzees, ~85% with mice, ~60% with bananas.

### The Human Body
- **Organ systems:** circulatory, respiratory, digestive, nervous, endocrine (hormones), immune, musculoskeletal, reproductive, urinary, integumentary (skin)
- **The brain** uses ~20% of the body's energy despite being ~2% of body weight.
- **Immune system** — Innate (general defenses) + adaptive (learns specific threats). Vaccines train the adaptive immune system.
- **Microbiome** — ~38 trillion bacteria live in/on your body (roughly equal to your own cell count). Gut bacteria affect digestion, mood, immune function.
- **Genetics** — Humans have ~20,000–25,000 genes across 23 pairs of chromosomes. Most traits are influenced by many genes plus environment.

## Neuroscience / Consciousness
**What we know:**
- Consciousness arises from brain activity. Damage to specific brain regions causes specific changes to experience.
- No scientific consensus on *how* or *why* physical brain processes produce subjective experience (the "hard problem of consciousness").

**Leading theories:**
- **Global Workspace Theory** — Consciousness is information broadcast widely across the brain
- **Integrated Information Theory (IIT)** — Consciousness corresponds to integrated information; anything that integrates information has some degree of experience
- **Higher-Order Theories** — Consciousness requires the brain to represent its own mental states

**Open questions:** Do animals have consciousness? Could AI? What happens during anesthesia? Why does sleep exist?

## Earth Science
- **Age of Earth:** ~4.5 billion years
- **Age of Universe:** ~13.8 billion years
- **Plate tectonics** — Earth's surface is broken into ~15 major plates that move, causing earthquakes, volcanoes, and mountain formation
- **Water cycle** — Evaporation, condensation, precipitation, runoff. 71% of Earth's surface is water; 97% is saltwater.
- **Atmosphere:** 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen, ~0.04% CO₂ (but rising due to fossil fuels — this drives climate change)

## Scientific Method
1. Observe something
2. Form a hypothesis (testable explanation)
3. Design experiment to test it
4. Collect data
5. Analyze results
6. Draw conclusions
7. Peer review and replication

A theory in science is not a guess — it's an explanation supported by extensive, repeated evidence (e.g., theory of gravity, theory of evolution, germ theory of disease).
