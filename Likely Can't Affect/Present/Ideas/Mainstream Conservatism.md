### Core Principles
1. **Limited Government**
   - Minimal government intervention in economy and personal life
   - Power should reside with states and individuals, not federal government
   - Government as servant, not master, of existing ways of life

2. **Individual Liberty & Responsibility**
   - Personal freedom essential to human flourishing
   - Individuals responsible for their own success/failure
   - Free markets and economic freedom (laissez-faire economics)

3. **Traditional Values**
   - Emphasis on family, church, and community as fundamental institutions
   - Moral order rooted in Judeo-Christian tradition
   - Preservation of established customs, practices, and social order

4. **Fiscal Responsibility**
   - Low taxes, balanced budgets, limited government spending
   - Opposition to welfare dependency; support for self-reliance
   - Free market capitalism over government economic intervention

5. **Strong National Defense**
   - Robust military to protect American sovereignty
   - America First foreign policy approach

6. **Rule of Law**
   - Constitutional originalism and strict construction
   - Protection of property rights
   - Law and order; support for police and enforcement

7. **Gradual Change over Revolution**
   - Skepticism of radical transformation and utopian schemes
   - Respect for tradition, precedent, and incremental reform
   - "If it ain't broke, don't fix it" philosophy

### Philosophical Foundation
- Rooted in Edmund Burke (18th century) and classical liberalism
- Belief in imperfect human nature (not perfectible through government)
- Organic conception of society (interconnected, not collection of individuals)
- Skepticism about centralized planning and reason in politics

### American Context
- Declaration of Independence and Constitution as guiding documents
- "All men are created equal" with unalienable rights
- Emphasis on states' rights and federalism
- Republican Party primary home since 1950s-1960s

### Contemporary Issues (2025)
**Support:** Border security, lower taxes, school choice, gun rights, religious liberty, reduced regulation, energy independence

**Oppose:** Large government programs, high taxes, gun control, excessive regulation, DEI mandates, abortion (pro-life)

### Note
Not all conservatives agree on all issues; conservatism encompasses various schools (constitutional, fiscal, social, national)
