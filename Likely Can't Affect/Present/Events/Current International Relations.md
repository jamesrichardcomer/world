## Military Alliances
**NATO (North Atlantic Treaty Organization)**
- Founded 1949; 32 members (Finland joined 2023, Sweden 2024)
- US, Canada, most of Europe, Turkey
- Article 5: Attack on one = attack on all
- Accounts for ~55% of global military spending
- Under pressure from Trump administration to increase member contributions

**CSTO (Collective Security Treaty Organization)**
- Russia-led; members include Armenia, Belarus, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Tajikistan
- Russia dominates; credibility weakened (failed to prevent Armenia-Azerbaijan conflict)

**SCO (Shanghai Cooperation Organisation)**
- China and Russia-led; includes India, Pakistan, Iran, Central Asian states
- Security + economic cooperation
- Could not prevent India-Pakistan escalation in 2025

**AUKUS**
- Australia, UK, US (2021)
- Nuclear submarine technology sharing; Indo-Pacific security focus

**Five Eyes**
- Intelligence sharing: US, UK, Canada, Australia, New Zealand

**ANZUS**
- Australia, New Zealand, US (1951)
- Mutual defense pact for the Pacific

**Quad (Quadrilateral Security Dialogue)**
- US, Japan, India, Australia
- Informal strategic forum; counterbalance to China in Indo-Pacific

## Economic Blocs & Trade Agreements
**G7** — US, UK, France, Germany, Italy, Canada, Japan
- Historically dominant industrial economies; informal policy coordination

**G20** — G7 + China, India, Brazil, Russia, Australia, South Korea, Mexico, Indonesia, Saudi Arabia, Turkey, Argentina, South Africa, EU
- Represents ~80% of world GDP

**BRICS** — Brazil, Russia, India, China, South Africa + expanded (Egypt, Ethiopia, Iran, UAE, Saudi Arabia, Indonesia joined 2024–2025)
- Positioned as alternative to Western-led institutions
- Growing influence; combined population exceeds half the world

**EU (European Union)** — 27 member states
- Single market, free movement, common currency (euro) for most
- Largest single market by GDP after US

**USMCA** — US, Mexico, Canada (replaced NAFTA in 2020)
- North American free trade; Trump threatening new tariffs on Canada/Mexico

**ASEAN** — 10 Southeast Asian nations
- Economic integration, security dialogue

**RCEP (Regional Comprehensive Economic Partnership)**
- Largest trade bloc by population: China, Japan, South Korea, Australia, New Zealand, ASEAN members
- Covers ~30% of global GDP

**African Union (AU)** — 55 member states
- Political and economic integration across Africa

## International Organizations
**United Nations**
- 193 member states
- Security Council: US, UK, France, Russia, China (permanent, with veto power) + 10 rotating
- Agencies: WHO, UNICEF, UNHCR, WFP, UNESCO, etc.

**WTO (World Trade Organization)**
- 164 members; governs international trade rules

**IMF (International Monetary Fund)**
- 190 members; financial stability, lending to countries in crisis

**World Bank**
- Development lending and poverty reduction

**ICC (International Criminal Court)**
- 124 member states; US, Russia, China, India are not members

## Major Sanctions Regimes (as of early 2026)
- **Russia** — Broad Western sanctions (US, EU, UK) over Ukraine invasion; energy, finance, technology
- **Iran** — US sanctions on oil, banking, nuclear program; Iran-Israel ceasefire in mid-2025 but tensions remain
- **North Korea** — UN, US, and multilateral sanctions over nuclear program
- **China** — US technology export controls (chips, AI); trade tensions ongoing
- **Venezuela, Syria, Myanmar** — Various targeted sanctions

---

## Key Dynamics
- **US-China rivalry** — Competition over technology, Taiwan, trade, influence in Global South
- **US under Trump** — Threatening territorial ambitions (Greenland, Panama); weakening multilateral norms; tariff escalation
- **Russia isolated from West** — But deepening ties with China, North Korea, Iran, Global South
- **Global South rising** — BRICS expansion; countries increasingly unwilling to align with either US or China exclusively
- **Multilateral system under strain** — UN, WTO, ICC all facing legitimacy and enforcement challenges
