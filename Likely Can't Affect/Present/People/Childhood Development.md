## Overview
Children develop across four main domains: physical (gross and fine motor), cognitive, language/communication, and social/emotional. These milestones represent what roughly 75% of children can do by a given age (per the CDC's 2022 updated standards). Every child develops at their own pace — these are guideposts, not rigid deadlines.

The brain grows the most during the first five years, reaching about 90% of its adult size. The head is nearly full-sized by age 1, and the rest of the body becomes more proportional throughout early childhood.

General pattern: Birth weight roughly triples by age 1. During year two, toddlers gain about 8 lbs and grow 4–5 inches. By year three, growth slows to about 4 lbs and 2–3 inches per year through the preschool years.

## 12 Months
**Physical**

- Pulls to stand
- Walks holding onto furniture (cruising)
- Picks things up with thumb and pointer finger (pincer grasp)
- Drinks from a cup without a lid when you hold it

**Cognitive**

- Puts things in a container (block in a cup)
- Looks for things you hide (object permanence developing)

**Language**

- Calls a parent "mama" or "dada" or another special name
- Understands "no" (pauses or stops briefly)

**Social/Emotional**

- Plays games like peek-a-boo and pat-a-cake
- Waves bye-bye

---

## 15 Months
**Physical**

- Takes a few steps on own (walking alone typically emerges here)

**Cognitive**

- Explores objects in different ways (shaking, banging, throwing)

**Language**

- Tries to say one or two words besides "mama" and "dada"
- Looks at a familiar object when you name it

**Social/Emotional**

- Claps when excited
- Hugs stuffed doll or toy
- Shows you an object they like

---

## 18 Months
**Physical**

- Walks without holding on to anyone or anything
- Scribbles
- Drinks from a cup without a lid (may spill)
- Feeds self with fingers; tries to use a spoon
- Climbs on and off a couch or chair without help

**Cognitive**

- Copies you doing chores (sweeping with a broom)
- Plays with toys in simple ways (pushing a toy car)

**Language**

- Tries to say three or more words besides "mama" and "dada"
- Follows one-step directions without gestures ("give it to me")

**Social/Emotional**

- Moves away from you but looks to make sure you're close by
- Points to show you something interesting
- Puts hands out for you to wash them
- Helps dress by pushing arm through sleeve or lifting foot

---

## 2 Years
**Physical**

- Kicks a ball
- Runs
- Walks (not climbs) up a few stairs with or without help
- Eats with a spoon

**Cognitive**

- Holds something in one hand while using the other (takes lid off container)
- Tries to use switches, knobs, or buttons on a toy
- Plays with more than one toy at the same time (puts toy food on toy plate)

**Language**

- Says at least two words together ("more milk")
- Points to at least two body parts when asked
- Uses gestures beyond waving and pointing (blowing a kiss, nodding yes)

**Social/Emotional**

- Notices when others are hurt or upset (pauses, looks sad when someone cries)
- Looks at your face to see how to react in a new situation

---

## 2.5 Years
**Physical**

- Uses hands to twist things (doorknobs, unscrewing lids)
- Takes some clothes off (loose pants, open jacket)
- Jumps off the ground with both feet
- Turns book pages one at a time

**Cognitive**

- Uses things to pretend (feeds a block to a doll like it's food)
- Shows simple problem-solving (stands on stool to reach something)
- Follows two-step instructions ("put the toy down and close the door")
- Shows knowledge of at least one color

**Language**

- Says about 50 words
- Says two or more words together with an action word ("doggie run")
- Names things in a book when you point and ask
- Says words like "I," "me," or "we"

**Social/Emotional**

- Plays next to other children and sometimes with them
- Shows you what they can do ("look at me!")
- Follows simple routines when told ("it's clean-up time")

---

## 3 Years
**Physical**

- Strings items together (large beads, macaroni)
- Puts on some clothes by self (loose pants, jacket)
- Uses a fork

**Cognitive**

- Draws a circle when you show how
- Avoids touching hot objects when warned

**Language**

- Talks in conversation with at least two back-and-forth exchanges
- Asks "who," "what," "where," and "why" questions
- Says what action is happening in a picture ("running," "eating")
- Says first name when asked
- Talks well enough for others to understand most of the time

**Social/Emotional**

- Calms down within 10 minutes after you leave (childcare drop-off)
- Notices other children and joins them to play

---

## 4 Years
**Physical**

- Catches a large ball most of the time
- Serves self food or pours water (with adult supervision)
- Unbuttons some buttons
- Holds crayon/pencil between fingers and thumb (not a fist)

**Cognitive**

- Names a few colors of items
- Tells what comes next in a well-known story
- Draws a person with three or more body parts

**Language**

- Says sentences with four or more words
- Says some words from a song, story, or nursery rhyme
- Talks about at least one thing that happened during the day
- Answers simple questions ("what is a coat for?")

**Social/Emotional**

- Pretends to be something else during play (teacher, superhero, dog)
- Asks to go play with children if none are around
- Comforts others who are hurt or sad (hugs a crying friend)
- Avoids danger (doesn't jump from tall heights at playground)
- Likes to be a "helper"
- Changes behavior based on where they are (library vs. playground)

---

## 5 Years
**Physical**

- Buttons some buttons
- Hops on one foot

**Cognitive**

- Counts to 10
- Names some numbers between 1 and 5 when pointed to
- Uses words about time ("yesterday," "tomorrow," "morning," "night")
- Pays attention for 5–10 minutes during activities (screen time doesn't count)
- Writes some letters in their name
- Names some letters when pointed to
- Uses or recognizes simple rhymes (bat-cat, ball-tall)

**Language**

- Tells a story with at least two events
- Answers questions about a book or story after hearing it
- Keeps a conversation going with more than three back-and-forth exchanges

**Social/Emotional**

- Follows rules or takes turns when playing games with other children
- Sings, dances, or acts for you
- Does simple chores at home (matching socks, clearing the table)

## Teeth
- By 12 months: typically 6–8 teeth
- By 2 years: most baby teeth are in (about 16)
- By 3 years: all 20 primary teeth are usually present
- First dental visit recommended by age 1 or when first tooth appears

## When to Talk to a Pediatrician
These are red flags at each stage, not diagnoses — but they warrant a conversation:

**By 12 months:** doesn't crawl, can't stand when supported, doesn't search for things you hide, doesn't say any single words, doesn't use gestures (waving, shaking head)

**By 18 months:** doesn't point to show things, doesn't walk, doesn't know what familiar things are for (cup, phone), doesn't have at least 6 words

**By 2 years:** doesn't use two-word phrases ("drink milk"), doesn't know what to do with common things (brush, phone, fork), doesn't copy actions or words, doesn't follow simple instructions

**By 3 years:** falls down a lot or has trouble with stairs, drools or has very unclear speech, can't work simple toys, doesn't speak in sentences, doesn't understand simple instructions, doesn't make-believe or pretend

**By 4 years:** can't jump in place, has trouble scribbling, shows no interest in interactive games or make-believe, ignores other children, resists dressing/sleeping/using the toilet, doesn't retell a favorite story

**By 5 years:** doesn't show a wide range of emotions, is extremely withdrawn, is easily distracted and can't focus on one activity for more than 5 minutes, doesn't respond to people, can't tell what's real vs. make-believe, doesn't play a variety of games

**At any age:** loses skills they once had (regression is always worth discussing immediately).
