## Federal Government Spending (Fiscal Year 2024)
**Total Spending:** $6.9 trillion (24% of GDP)  
**Total Revenue:** $4.9 trillion  
**Deficit:** $1.8 trillion (6.4% of GDP)

---

## Major Spending Categories
1. **Social Security ($1.5 trillion, 22%)**
   - Retirement and disability benefits for eligible Americans, governed by permanent law.

2. **Medicare ($972 billion, 14%)**
   - Health insurance for Americans 65+ and certain disabled individuals.

3. **Medicaid & CHIP ($616 billion, 9%)**
   - Health coverage for low-income individuals and children.

4. **Net Interest on Debt ($892 billion, 13%)**
   - Interest payments on accumulated federal debt, up 34% from 2023 due to higher interest rates.

5. **Defense ($872 billion, 13%)**
   - Department of Defense operations, personnel, weapons procurement, and research.

6. **Veterans Benefits ($526 billion, 8%)**
   - Disability payments, specialized medical care, and benefits for 18+ million veterans.

7. **Income Security ($508 billion, 7%)**
   - Unemployment, supplemental nutrition assistance (SNAP), housing assistance, and tax credits.

8. **Education & Training ($305 billion, 4%)**
   - Federal student aid, K-12 programs, job training, and research grants.

9. **Transportation ($154 billion, 2%)**
   - Highway maintenance, aviation, rail infrastructure, and public transit.

10. **Other Mandatory Programs ($1.1 trillion, 16%)**
    - Federal retirement, earned income tax credit, nutrition programs, and other entitlements.

---

## Spending Structure
**Mandatory Spending (61%):** $4.2 trillion
- Programs required by law (Social Security, Medicare, Medicaid)
- Operates on "autopilot" unless Congress changes underlying laws
- Has grown from 45% of budget in 1980 to 61% in 2024

**Discretionary Spending (26%):** $1.8 trillion
- Subject to annual congressional appropriations
- Defense: ~50% of discretionary ($872B)
- Non-defense: ~50% of discretionary ($872B)

**Net Interest (13%):** $892 billion
- Cost of servicing accumulated debt from 1789 to present
- Growing rapidly due to higher interest rates and expanding debt

---

## Key Trends
- Spending decreased 6.7% from FY 2023 to FY 2024 (pandemic programs ended)
- Up 23.8% from pre-pandemic FY 2019
- Per capita spending: $19,900 per person
- Mandatory spending continues consuming larger share of budget each year
- Interest payments surged 34% year-over-year as rates climbed
