### Elevation
**Average:** 79-98 feet (24-30m) above sea level  
**Range:** 50-100 feet typical (some areas -7 to 322 feet)  
**Highest in Orange County:** Winter Garden area (222 feet / 68m near Lake County line)

### Terrain
**Predominantly flat** with gentle hills and low-lying areas
- Characterized by gentle to moderately sloping topography
- Despite flatness, includes noticeable gentle elevation changes
- Sandy soils throughout

### Water Features
**Numerous lakes** - defining characteristic of Orlando landscape
- Lake Apopka (major lake northwest)
- Lake Eola (downtown icon)
- Butler Chain of Lakes (Windermere area)
- Hundreds of smaller lakes scattered throughout

**Wetlands & Marshes:**
- Extensive due to presence of lakes and rivers
- Contributes to diverse ecosystem
- Important for water management

**Rivers:**
- Proximity to various waterways and drainage systems

### Geographic Context
**Location:** Central Florida peninsula
- 42 miles inland from Atlantic Ocean
- 77 miles inland from Gulf of Mexico
- Low elevation and peninsula position contribute to climate

**Regional Classification:**
- Part of Florida Uplands physiographic region (central Florida)
- Contrasts with coastal plains to east and west
- Rolling hills and pine forests in broader region (though Orlando itself quite flat)

### Landscape Character
- Pockets of greenery around numerous lakes
- Interspersed sandy soils
- Generally flat allows for easy development and sprawl
- Lake features provide natural beauty and recreation opportunities
