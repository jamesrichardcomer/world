# Major Georgetown Roads
## I. Primary Highways & Interstates
### A. Interstate 35 (I-35)
- **Description:** The primary north-south artery that bisects Georgetown, splitting the city into east and west sections.
    
- **Function:** Connects Georgetown directly to Austin (South), Temple/Waco (North), and serves as the main commuter route.
    
- **Key Features:**
    
    - **The Double-Deck:** Unlike Austin, Georgetown's stretch is surface-level but elevates over major cross-streets (University Ave, Williams Dr).
        
    - **Congestion:** Highly prone to bottlenecks during rush hour (7–9 AM southbound, 4–7 PM northbound), particularly near the SH 29 and Williams Drive exits.
        

### B. State Highway 130 (SH 130) - "The Toll Road"
- **Description:** A high-speed toll highway running along the eastern edge of Georgetown.
    
- **Function:** Acts as a bypass for I-35 traffic and provides the fastest route to **Austin-Bergstrom International Airport (AUS)** and the Tesla Giga Texas facility.
    
- **Speed Limit:** Notable for having the highest speed limit in the U.S. (85 mph on southern segments).
    
- **Use Case:** Best for avoiding I-35 gridlock if you are willing to pay the toll.
    

### C. State Highway 29 (University Avenue)
- **Description:** The primary east-west corridor running directly through the center of Georgetown.
    
- **Westbound:** Leads into the Hill Country (Burnet, Llano) and Lake Georgetown.
    
- **Eastbound:** Leads to Circleville and connects to SH 130.
    
- **Key Landmarks:** Passes directly in front of **Southwestern University** and intersects with the historic downtown square.
    
- **Traffic Note:** The "University Ave" stretch near I-35 is one of the busiest intersections in the city.
    

### D. State Highway 195
- **Description:** A major highway branching northwest from I-35.
    
- **Function:** The primary connector between Georgetown and **Killeen/Fort Hood**.
    
- **Development:** Rapidly developing corridor with increasing residential subdivisions (e.g., Berry Creek area).
    

## II. Major Arterials & Thoroughfares
### A. Williams Drive
- **Description:** The most critical commercial corridor, extending northwest from I-35 towards Sun City.
    
- **Character:** Lined with the majority of Georgetown’s retail, grocery stores (H-E-B), and banks.
    
- **Significance:** Serves as the primary "Gateway" to the massive **Sun City** retirement community.
    
- **Traffic:** Heavy volume; notoriously difficult to turn left across traffic due to the number of curb cuts and businesses.
    

### B. Austin Avenue
- **Description:** The historic north-south spine of the city, running parallel to I-35.
    
- **North Austin Ave:** Connects the Square to Williams Drive and older residential areas.
    
- **South Austin Ave:** Runs south towards Round Rock, passing older commercial districts.
    
- **Significance:** Passes directly through the **Historic Town Square** (The "Most Beautiful Town Square in Texas").
    

### C. The "Inner Loop" (SE Inner Loop / NE Inner Loop)
- **Description:** A partial ring road that circles the eastern and southern portions of the city.
    
- **Function:** Allows traffic to bypass the downtown square and University Ave congestion.
    
- **Key Landmarks:** Williamson County Justice Center, Sheriff's Office, and various county annex buildings.
    

### D. DB Wood Road
- **Description:** A major north-south arterial on the **west side** of Georgetown.
    
- **Function:** Connects Williams Drive (North) to University Ave/SH 29 (South) and extends further toward Cedar Park.
    
- **Key Features:** Runs adjacent to the San Gabriel River and provides access to Georgetown High School and several parks.
    

### E. Ronald Reagan Blvd (Parmer Lane)
- **Description:** A massive north-south arterial located in **far West Georgetown**.
    
- **Function:** Connects Georgetown to Leander and Cedar Park, serving as a western alternative to I-35.
    
- **Development:** The primary corridor for "Santa Rita Ranch" and other massive new master-planned communities.
    

## III. Secondary & Connector Roads
- **Leander Road (RM 2243):** Runs west from I-35, connecting Georgetown to Leander. A mix of older homes and new rapid development.
    
- **Wolf Ranch Parkway:** A short but vital loop on the west side serving the **Wolf Ranch Town Center** (Target, Best Buy, Dining).
    
- **FM 971:** Runs east from downtown (near the library) towards Weir and Granger; passes San Gabriel Park.
    
- **FM 1460:** A recently widened north-south road on the east side, connecting Georgetown to Round Rock, passing the hospitals (Baylor Scott & White).
    

## IV. Traffic "Hot Spots" to Watch
1. **I-35 & Hwy 29 Intersection:** Almost always congested; the underpass turnarounds can back up significantly.
    
2. **Williams Drive & I-35:** A major bottleneck due to the volume of traffic entering/exiting the highway and Sun City residents commuting.
    
3. **The Square:** Austin Ave traffic slows to a crawl around the courthouse square; pedestrian heavy.
